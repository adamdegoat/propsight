The month in brief
Private

The private resale median is $1,753 psf, up 0.6% on the month and 3.8% on the year, with a typical deal around $1.65m. It rests on 22,196 transactions over 18 months, and the latest month is preliminary.

Segments

OCR suburbs are at $1,523 psf, up 1.4% over three months, while city-fringe RCR at $1,962 psf has slowed to 0.6%. Prime CCR is $2,203 psf, down 2.4%. The strength is in the mass market, not the top end.

HDB

The HDB resale median is $605 psf, up 0.3% on the year, but the averages hide fast movers. Marine Parade is up 11.0% over three months, Toa Payoh 10.6%, the Central Area 9.5% and Bukit Merah 6.4%, while Geylang has slipped 7.5% and Kallang/Whampoa 4.8%. On flat size, expect roughly $446k for a 3-room, $630k for a 4-room, $740k for a 5-room and $910k for an executive.

Rental

A private home rents for a median $4,300 a month at a 3.6% gross yield. An HDB flat rents for $3,200 a month at a 6.1% yield, a stronger cash return.

New launches

Buyers pay a premium for new. OCR launches such as Chuan Park at $2,598 psf, Parktown Residence at $2,361 psf and Springleaf Residence at $2,169 psf sit well above the $1,523 psf for suburban resale, and RCR launches run higher, with One Marina Gardens at $2,957 psf and The Orie at $2,723 psf.

Policy

Nothing changed in Budget 2026. Second-home ABSD stays at 20% for citizens and 60% for foreigners, the Seller's Stamp Duty holds to the tighter four-year window set in July 2025, at 16% if you sell within a year, and TDSR remains 55% with a 75% loan-to-value limit on a first bank loan. A wave of flats reaching their five-year MOP over the next year should add resale supply.

New launches are selling well above the resale market next door. In the OCR, Parktown Residence is at $2,361 psf, Springleaf Residence at $2,169 psf, Chuan Park at $2,598 psf and Tengah Garden Residences at $2,113 psf, against a suburban resale benchmark of $1,523 psf. On the city fringe, The Orie is at $2,723 psf and One Marina Gardens at $2,957 psf, well above the RCR resale median of $1,962 psf.

That gap covers the developer's land cost, construction and margin, and buyers pay today for a home that completes years from now. Buying new is a bet that resale prices rise enough to close the gap by the time you sell, and with resale barely moving and an en bloc pipeline from Chuan Park at $890.00m, Loyang Valley at $880.00m and Thiam Siew Avenue at $815.00m adding future supply, that is not a safe assumption. The rental market has come off its peak. A private home lets for a median $4,300 a month, a 3.6% gross yield against today's prices. After borrowing, maintenance and the odd vacant month, that is a thin return on the money tied up.

HDB pays better. A flat rents for a median $3,200 a month, and because the entry price is far lower, the gross yield is 6.1%. For cash flow rather than capital gain, HDB is the stronger choice.

What this means for landlords

Put money into HDB for the 6.1% yield rather than private at 3.6%, and price to fill quickly now that rents have eased.

Supply & en bloc

The pipeline behind 2027 and 2028 prices

Three collective sales have just cleared: Chuan Park at $890.00m, Loyang Valley at $880.00m and Thiam Siew Avenue at $815.00m. Each site will return as new homes, but not soon, so they do nothing to ease supply today.

On the public side, a wave of HDB flats reaches its five-year minimum occupation period over the coming year, adding resale flats to the market. Together, the redevelopment and the freed-up flats bring extra supply in 2027 and 2028, not now. That pipeline sits two to three years out and is the main reason to expect a cap on this run rather than a break today.

Rates & policy

Cooling measures intact, rates in charge

Nothing has moved. Budget 2026 made no changes to housing. Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty is 20% on a citizen's second home and 60% for a foreigner. Seller's Stamp Duty was tightened in July 2025 to a four-year holding window, with a 16% charge if you sell inside the first year.

The borrowing limits hold: total debt servicing is capped at 55% of income, and a first bank loan is limited to 75% of the property value. With policy settled, interest rates now move the market. Cheaper money lifts what buyers can pay, dearer money pulls it back.

The month ahead

Outlook

Base case for the month ahead is a slow grind. Private resale is at a median $1,753 psf, up 0.6% on the month and 3.8% on the year, and nothing in the numbers points to a sudden break either way. The suburbs are leading, up 1.4% over three months, while prime is down 2.4%, so the market stays uneven.

The risks cut both ways. On the upside, a rate cut would give buyers more spending power and prices could firm faster than the recent pace. On the downside, the supply building for 2027 and 2028, plus a prime segment already slipping, means the top end could stay weak and drag the headline number if buyers turn cautious.

Our call

Our call: expect prices to keep rising in the suburbs while prime lags, so buy where you intend to live and let yield, not the hope of a quick flip, carry the decision.
