A record number of flats can finally be sold this year. Does that make them cheaper?今年终于有创纪录数量的组屋可以出售。这会让它们更便宜吗？

Every agency is quoting the same number: around 13,480 flats reach their five-year mark in 2026, roughly double last year. Nobody seems to have checked whether that actually makes them cheaper. We used our own transaction records to find out.各家房产中介引用的都是同一个数字：2026年约有13,480个组屋单位届满五年期限，约为去年的两倍。但似乎没有人核实过，这是否真的会让房价变得更便宜。我们利用自有的成交数据，找出了答案。

The short answer简短回答

It barely moves the price. Flats that have just passed their five-year mark do sell for about 20% more per square foot than the rest of their town, which sounds like the opposite of a supply glut. But those flats also have around 95 years of lease left. Compare them only against other long-lease flats in the same town and flat type and the premium shrinks to about 2%. Roughly nine tenths of what looks like a freshness premium is simply lease. There is a small buyer's edge where the wave is genuinely thick. In Tampines these flats sell about 2.5% below comparable young flats and in Bukit Batok about 3.0% below. That is worth a few percent rather than the double digit discount the headlines imply.影响微乎其微。刚满五年的组屋，其每平方英尺售价确实比同镇其他单位高出约20%，这听起来与供应过剩的情形恰恰相反。但这些单位的剩余地契也长达约95年。若只将它们与同镇、同房型且剩余地契同样较长的单位相比，溢价便收窄至约2%。换言之，看似"房龄新"带来的溢价，约有九成其实只是地契年限。在供应确实集中的市镇，买家确有一点议价空间。在淡滨尼，这类单位的成交价比条件相近的新地契单位低约2.5%，武吉巴督则低约3.0%。这相当于几个百分点的让利，而非新闻标题所暗示的两位数折扣。

The number everyone is quoting is not HDB's人人引用的那个数字，并非出自建屋发展局

Start with the figure itself. Around 13,480 flats are said to reach the end of their five-year Minimum Occupation Period this year, against about 6,970 in 2025. That number is an industry compilation, not an HDB release. ERA, PropNex and OrangeTee each publish their own version from public completion data, and they do not agree: you will see 13,480 and 13,484, a 2025 base of 6,970 and 6,973, and Queenstown at both 2,405 and 2,409.

The town breakdown is looser still. The widely repeated Toa Payoh figure of 1,594 flats is the exact unit count of one project, Alkaff Oasis in Bidadari, which suggests these town totals are really project completion counts added up. One major portal lists a completely different set of towns for the same 2026 cohort. None of this makes the wave imaginary, and the direction is clearly right. It just means the precision is borrowed.

What struck us is that every source we read states the price effect as an expectation rather than a finding. More flats will mean more choice, so buyers will have room to negotiate. It sounds obvious. We could not find a single published analysis testing it against past transactions, so we ran one.

先从这个数字本身说起。据称今年约有13,480个单位届满五年最低居住年限（MOP），而2025年约为6,970个。这一数字属于行业整理，而非建屋发展局发布。ERA、PropNex和OrangeTee各自根据公开的竣工资料编制自己的版本，且彼此并不一致：你会看到13,480与13,484两种说法，2025年的基数有6,970和6,973之分，女皇镇则同时出现2,405和2,409两个数字。

分市镇的数据则更为松散。被广泛引用的大巴窑1,594个单位这一数字，恰好是单一项目, , 位于碧山亚逸的Alkaff Oasis, , 的确切单位总数，这说明所谓的市镇总量，其实是把各项目的竣工数量相加而成。某大型房产门户网站针对同一批2026年届满群体，列出的市镇名单则完全不同。这些并不意味着供应潮子虚乌有，方向显然是对的，只是那份精确度是借来的。

令我们意外的是，我们读到的每一份资料，都把价格影响表述为一种预期，而非一项研究结论。供应增加意味着选择变多，因此买家将有议价空间。这听上去理所当然。但我们找不到任何一份已发表的分析用过往成交数据加以检验，于是我们自己做了一次。

The premium that turns out to be a lease所谓溢价，其实是地契

We took every block in our data with a lease that started in 2020 or 2021, the flats that have just crossed the five-year mark, and compared their resale prices against the rest of their town. Four-room and five-room flats only, 13 towns, 1,739 sales against 3,425 comparison sales, from April 2025 to June 2026.

On the face of it these flats are expensive, not cheap. The median newly eligible flat sold about 20% higher per square foot than the typical flat in the same town. If a supply wave were dragging prices down, this is not what it would look like.

Then we controlled for the one thing that obviously differs. A flat that just hit its five-year mark has around 95 years of lease left, while the town median is far shorter. Compare the same flats only against other flats in the same town and flat type with at least 87 years remaining, and the 20% premium falls to about 2%. We tested the cutoff at 85 and 90 years and got the same answer. Almost the entire premium was the lease, not the flat.

That is the useful part. If you are looking at a just-past-MOP flat and telling yourself the price is justified because it is barely lived in, the market does not agree with you. It is paying for the lease, which you can check on any flat, new or not.

我们选取了数据中所有地契起始于2020或2021年的座数，即刚跨过五年门槛的单位，并将其转售价格与同镇其余单位作比较。范围仅限四房式和五房式单位，涵盖13个市镇，共1,739宗成交与3,425宗对照成交，时间跨度为2025年4月至2026年6月。

表面看来，这些单位是贵而非便宜。刚届满资格的单位，每平方英尺中位成交价比同镇典型单位高出约20%。若真有一波供应正在压低价格，情形不会是这样。

接着，我们控制了那个显而易见的差异变量。刚满五年的单位剩余地契约95年，而全镇中位数则短得多。若只将同一批单位与同镇、同房型且剩余地契至少87年的单位相比，那20%的溢价便降至约2%。我们把门槛分别设在85年和90年重新检验，结果一致。几乎全部溢价来自地契，而非房子本身。

这才是有用的部分。如果你正在看一套刚满五年的单位，并说服自己"价格合理，因为几乎没人住过"，市场其实并不认同你。市场付的是地契的钱, , 而地契，无论新旧，任何单位都查得到。

Where the wave does show up, a little供应潮确有显现，但幅度有限

The supply story is not entirely absent, it is just much smaller than advertised. Rank the towns by how many newly eligible flats actually changed hands in our records, and the places where most of that stock is trading are the places where it sells at a discount. Tampines, with 434 such sales, is 2.5% below comparable long-lease flats. Bukit Batok, with 247, is 3.0% below. Clementi, with 62, sits 12.8% above.

Split the towns in half and the pattern is the same: the more active half sits at about 0.7% and the quieter half at about 3.2%. In Tampines and Bukit Batok the discount also widened slightly over the year we measured. It is a consistent tilt in the direction you would expect, across only 13 towns, and we would not build a decision on it by itself. But it is worth a few percent, and it is the real thing people are gesturing at.

One more check from the other direction. If the wave were flooding the market, volumes should be up. They are not. HDB's own figures show 12,553 resale transactions in the first half of 2026, down 8.3% from 13,692 a year earlier, while the resale price index fell 0.1% in the first quarter and a further 0.3% in the second. Prices are easing and volumes are easing together, which is a market cooling for its own reasons, not one being flooded with flats. ERA credits the 15 month wait-out rule and heavier BTO supply rather than the MOP cohort, and on this evidence that reads as the better explanation.

供应的故事并非完全不存在，只是远比宣传的要小。若按各市镇实际成交的"刚届满资格"单位数量排序，会发现这类房源交投最活跃的地方，正是其成交价出现折让的地方。淡滨尼有434宗此类成交，价格比条件相近的长地契单位低2.5%；武吉巴督有247宗，低3.0%；金文泰仅62宗，反而高出12.8%。

将各市镇对半划分，规律也是一致的：交投较活跃的一半约为0.7%，较冷清的一半约为3.2%。在淡滨尼和武吉巴督，折让幅度在我们观察的这一年中还略有扩大。这是一个方向符合预期的稳定倾向，但样本仅13个市镇，我们不会单凭这一点做决策。不过它确实值几个百分点，也正是人们所指的那个真实现象。

再从另一个角度做一次验证。如果真有供应潮涌入市场，成交量理应上升。事实并非如此。建屋发展局自身的数据显示，2026年上半年转售成交量为12,553宗，较一年前的13,692宗下跌8.3%；同期转售价格指数第一季度下滑0.1%，第二季度再跌0.3%。价格与成交量同步走软，这是一个因自身原因而降温的市场，而非被大量房源淹没的市场。ERA将成因归于15个月等待期规定以及预购组屋（BTO）供应增加，而非届满最低居住年限的这批房源；就现有证据而言，这确实是更站得住脚的解释。

What this means for you这对你意味着什么

If you are buying, do not wait for a wave of cheap flats, because it is not showing up in the prices. Judge the flat on its remaining lease and its block, not on how recently it passed the five-year mark, since that is what the market is actually pricing. If you are shopping in Tampines or Bukit Batok you have a little more room than elsewhere, so use it, but budget for a few percent rather than a bargain. If you are selling a flat that has just become eligible, there is no reason to rush the listing out of fear that your neighbours will undercut you. In the towns with the heaviest supply the gap is about 3%, and the wider market is soft on prices and quieter on volume, so price to what your block has actually transacted at in recent months rather than to last year's peak. And whichever side you are on, treat the 13,480 figure as a rough industry estimate, because HDB does not publish one.如果你打算买房，不必苦等一波便宜房源，因为价格上根本看不到这种迹象。请依据单位的剩余地契和所在座数来判断其价值，而不是看它刚满五年多久，因为这才是市场真正在定价的东西。若你在淡滨尼或武吉巴督看房，回旋余地会比其他地方稍大，不妨善加利用，但应按几个百分点的幅度做预算，而非期待捡便宜。如果你打算出售刚届满五年的单位，也没有理由担心邻居抢先低价出售而急于挂牌。即便在供应量最大的市镇，价差也仅约3%，而且当前大市价格偏软、成交量趋淡，因此定价应参照你所在座数近几个月的实际成交价，而非去年的高点。无论你身处买方还是卖方，都应把13,480这个数字视作一个粗略的行业估算，因为建屋发展局并未公布相关数据。
