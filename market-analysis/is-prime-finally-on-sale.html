Is prime finally on sale?优质地段终于降价了吗？

For the first time in years, the prime core is the only part of the Singapore market where prices are slipping. We looked at whether that is a genuine opening, or just a headline.多年来第一次，优质核心区成为新加坡市场上唯一价格出现回落的板块。我们探讨了这究竟是一个真正的入市良机，还是仅仅是一个吸引眼球的标题。

The short answer简短回答

Yes, in a narrow sense. Core Central is the only segment where prices are falling, down about 2.4% over the last three months while the suburbs and city fringe keep climbing, so a Singaporean buying to hold can negotiate in prime for the first time in years. But it is an easing, not a fire sale: scarce central land, a thin launch pipeline and steady local demand put a floor under how far it drops. And for a foreign buyer, the 60% stamp duty still swamps any discount on the table.从狭义上说，是的。核心中央区（CCR）是唯一价格下跌的板块，过去三个月下跌约2.4%，而郊区和市区边缘地带仍在持续攀升，因此以持有为目的的新加坡买家多年来第一次可以在优质地段进行议价。但这只是趋于平缓，而非清仓甩卖：稀缺的中央区土地、稀薄的新盘供应管道以及稳定的本地需求，为跌幅设下了底线。而对于外国买家而言，60%的印花税仍然远远抵消了任何摆在桌面上的折扣。

Prime is the only part of the market falling优质地段是市场上唯一下跌的板块

Split the private market into its three rings and the picture is unusually clear. The suburbs (OCR) and the city fringe (RCR) are still rising. The prime core (CCR), the traditional luxury districts, is the one segment going the other way, down about 2.4% in median price per square foot over the most recent three months.

That is a real reversal. For most of the last few years prime led the market up. Now it is the laggard, while the mass market it used to sit above does the leading.

将私宅市场划分为三个圈层来看，情况格外清晰。郊区（OCR）和市区边缘地带（RCR）仍在上涨。而优质核心区（CCR），即传统的豪宅地段，是唯一走势相反的板块，最近三个月的每平方英尺中位价下跌约2.4%。

这是一次真正的逆转。在过去几年的大部分时间里，优质地段一直引领市场上行。如今它却成了落后者，而曾经居于其下的大众市场反而担当起领涨的角色。

Why prime cooled while the suburbs did not为何优质地段降温，而郊区却没有

The prime core used to be priced by international buyers. The 60% Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty on foreigners, in place since 2023, quietly cut that demand off. What is left is domestic: citizens and returning Singaporeans, and they buy across the whole island, not just the core.

So prime lost the buyer that set its price, while the suburbs kept the buyer that sets theirs. That is the whole story in one line. You can still see the gap it leaves: prime resale asks about 45% more per square foot than the suburbs, for an address a thinner pool of buyers now competes for.

优质核心区的定价过去由国际买家主导。自2023年起实施的针对外国人的60%额外买方印花税（ABSD），悄然切断了这部分需求。剩下的是本地需求：本国公民和归国的新加坡人，而他们的置业范围遍及全岛，并不局限于核心区。

因此，优质地段失去了为其定价的买家，而郊区则保住了为其定价的买家。一句话便道尽了全部缘由。你仍然可以看到这一变化所留下的差距：优质地段的转售叫价每平方英尺比郊区高出约45%，而如今争夺这一地段的买家群体却更为稀薄。

How far can it really fall它究竟能跌多深

Not far, on the evidence. Official URA figures show the prime core flat to slightly lower, its softest run since 2024, not a collapse. Three things hold the floor up: central land is scarce and expensive, the new-launch pipeline in the core is thin, and domestic upgrader and ultra-wealthy demand has not gone away.

So read "on sale" as a few percent of give from sellers who need to move, in a slow, thinly traded market, not a discount rack. The opening is in the negotiation, not in a falling market you can time.

从现有证据来看，跌幅有限。市区重建局（URA）的官方数据显示，优质核心区价格持平至略有下降，是自2024年以来最疲软的一段走势，但绝非崩盘。有三个因素支撑着底部：中央区土地稀缺且昂贵、核心区的新盘供应管道稀薄，以及本地换房买家和超高净值人群的需求并未消失。

因此，应把“降价”理解为在一个缓慢、成交清淡的市场中，急于脱手的卖家做出的几个百分点的让步，而非折扣货架。机会存在于议价之中，而非一个你可以踩点抄底的下跌市场。

What this means for you这对你意味着什么

If you are a Singaporean buying to live in or hold for years, this is a rare moment to negotiate in prime. Look for resale where the seller needs to move, and sanity-check the price against the suburb premium, you are paying roughly 45% more per square foot for the address. If you are buying to flip, a slow, thin prime market is the wrong place to be. And if you are a foreign buyer, the 60% stamp duty still dwarfs anything you will save on price.如果你是以自住或长期持有为目的的新加坡买家，这是一个难得的可以在优质地段议价的时机。留意那些卖家急于脱手的转售房源，并将价格与郊区溢价进行合理性对照, , 为了这个地段，你每平方英尺大约要多付45%。如果你是以转手套利为目的的买家，一个缓慢而清淡的优质市场并非合适之地。而如果你是外国买家，60%的印花税仍然远远超过你在价格上所能省下的任何金额。
