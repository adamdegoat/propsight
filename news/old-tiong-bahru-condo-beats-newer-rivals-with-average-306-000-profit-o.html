Old Tiong Bahru Condo Beats Newer Rivals With Average $306,000 Profit on 2-BeddersOld Tiong Bahru Condo Beats Newer Rivals With Average $306,000 Profit on 2-Bedders

$306,000 average profit per 2-bedderaverage profit per 2-bedder

An older condominium in Tiong Bahru has quietly outrun its younger competition when it comes to resale profits. Analysis of recent transactions shows sellers of 2-bedroom units there walked away with an average gain of $306,000 per unit, beating out newer developments across the Bukit Merah and Queenstown districts.

The result challenges the common assumption that a newer building automatically delivers better returns. In mature estates like Tiong Bahru, strong demand is driven by the neighbourhood itself: the heritage character, the amenities, the transport links. These pull in buyers regardless of how old the building is.

If you own or are considering buying a 2-bedder in this part of town, focus on what you paid or plan to pay and on the specific address, not the year the building was completed. A well-located older unit bought at the right price will almost always outperform a newer one bought at the wrong one.
