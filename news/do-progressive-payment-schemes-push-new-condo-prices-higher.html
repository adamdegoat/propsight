Do Progressive Payment Schemes Push New Condo Prices Higher?分期付款方式会推高新condo售价吗？

A Business Times analysis has put forward a pointed question: do progressive payment schemes, where new condo buyers pay in stages as their unit is being built, give developers cover to set higher launch prices? The argument is that when the total cost is spread over several years of construction, the full price feels less immediate, making buyers more willing to sign at higher numbers.

This is a well-established idea in behavioural economics. When you are not writing one large cheque upfront, the sticker shock is reduced. If staged payments consistently make buyers more comfortable committing at higher price points, developers have little reason to hold back on their launch pricing. The payment structure, in other words, may be doing some of the selling for them.

For anyone weighing a new launch purchase against a resale alternative right now, this is a useful prompt to slow down and run the full numbers. The total price and the total financing cost over the loan period are what matter, not how manageable the payment schedule feels in the early years. Lay both options out on a like-for-like basis, including interest during the construction period, before deciding which is genuinely better value.

《商业时报》的一篇分析提出了一个值得深思的问题：分期付款方式，即新condo买家在单位建造过程中分阶段付款，是否给了发展商提高发售价格的空间？论点在于，当总费用分摊在数年建设期内，全额价格的冲击感就会减弱，让买家更愿意在较高的价位上签约。

这是行为经济学中一个成熟的概念。当你不需要一次性开出一张大额支票时，价格标签的震撼感就会降低。如果分阶段付款方式能持续让买家在较高价位上更容易做出承诺，发展商就没有理由压低发售价格。换句话说，付款结构本身或许已经在帮助销售了。

对于正在权衡新项目与二手房选择的买家，这是一个有用的提醒，让你放慢脚步、认真算清全部数字。真正重要的是总价以及贷款期内的总融资成本，而不是早期付款计划看起来有多轻松。在做决定之前，把两个选择按同等条件摆开来比较，包括建设期内的利息，看看哪一个真正更划算。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
