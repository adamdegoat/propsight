Weaker jobs outlook is dragging down HDB resale prices and deal volumes in Q2就业前景转弱，拖累HDB转售价格与成交量在Q2进一步走软

Job worries are showing up directly in the HDB resale numbers. In Q2 2026, both the number of transactions and prices softened further, as buyers grew more cautious about taking on large financial commitments when the employment outlook feels shaky.

HDB resale flats are typically purchased by Singaporeans using CPF savings and housing grants, so household income stability matters a great deal to this group. When buyers are less certain about their jobs, they either delay the purchase decision entirely or push back harder on the asking price.

For buyers currently in the market, this shift works in your favour. You have a little more negotiating room than you would have had a year ago. For sellers, the message is that realistic pricing from the start matters more now than hoping a desperate buyer will stretch to your number.

对工作前景的忧虑，正在直接反映在HDB转售数据上。2026年Q2，成交量和价格双双进一步走软，买家在就业前景不明朗的情况下，对承担大额财务承诺变得更加谨慎。

HDB转售组屋的买家通常依赖CPF储蓄和购房补贴，因此家庭收入的稳定性对这个群体来说至关重要。当买家对工作的保障感不足时，他们要么干脆推迟购买决定，要么在出价上更用力地压低卖家的开价。

对于目前正在看房的买家来说，这一转变对你有利。你比一年前多了一点讨价还价的空间。对于卖家而言，现在的情况是，从一开始就定出切实际的价格，比期待一个急迫的买家主动出高价，来得重要得多。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
