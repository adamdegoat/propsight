Most Buyers of Million-Dollar HDB Flats Paid No Cash Above Valuation in 20252025年，大多数百万组屋买家无需支付高于估价的额外现金

$0 cash above valuation most buyers paid大多数买家支付的高于估价现金额

A common worry among buyers of expensive HDB resale flats is the cash-over-valuation gap, the difference between what you agree to pay the seller and what the bank or HDB actually values the flat at. If valuation falls short of the price, you have to cover the difference in cash. PropNex data from 2025 shows that across million-dollar HDB deals, most buyers did not face this problem at all.

For the majority of those transactions, valuations kept pace with the transaction price. That meant buyers could finance the purchase through CPF savings and a bank loan in the normal way, without needing to come up with extra cash just to plug a valuation gap. This is a more reassuring picture than many buyers assume going in.

The practical point is that buying a more expensive HDB resale flat does not automatically mean you need a large cash buffer sitting on top of your usual down payment. That said, individual flats still vary, and you should always check the valuation on the specific unit you want before signing anything. What is true across the market on average does not guarantee the same result for every deal.

购买价格较高的HDB转售组屋，买家最常担心的是超额现金（cash-over-valuation）的问题，也就是你同意支付给卖家的价格，与银行或HDB实际估价之间的差额。如果估价低于成交价，这个差额必须用现金补足。PropNex的2025年数据显示，在百万组屋交易中，大多数买家根本没有遇到这个问题。

在这些交易的大多数案例中，估价基本上跟上了成交价。这意味着买家可以通过正常方式，用CPF储蓄和银行贷款，来完成购买，无需为填补估价差额额外筹备现金。这比许多买家事前设想的情况要令人放心得多。

实际的意思是：购买较贵的HDB转售组屋，并不自动意味着你需要在常规首付之外再备一大笔现金。话虽如此，个别组屋的情况仍会有所不同，在签署任何文件之前，你都应该先核实你心仪的那套单位的估价。市场整体的平均情况，并不保证每笔交易都能得到相同结果。
