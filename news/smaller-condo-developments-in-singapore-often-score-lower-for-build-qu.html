Smaller Condo Developments in Singapore Often Score Lower for Build Quality新加坡较小型condo项目的建筑质量评分普遍偏低

If you have ever been drawn to a small boutique condo over a sprawling 500-unit development, there is a trade-off worth knowing. An analysis of construction quality assessments in Singapore found that smaller condo projects consistently score lower on build quality than larger ones. The gap is not random noise. It tracks closely enough to suggest something structural is going on.

The most likely explanation is economies of scale. A large development can justify a full-time site supervisor dedicated solely to quality control, bulk procurement of materials from established suppliers, and tighter oversight at every stage of construction. A smaller project often cannot spread those costs the same way, so corners get cut, not necessarily from bad faith, but because the economics are harder.

For buyers, this is a practical flag, not a reason to rule out boutique condos entirely. A small project from a developer with a strong track record is very different from one being delivered by a first-timer or a developer with complaints on record. Before you commit, look up the developer's past projects and check whether any quality assessment scores are publicly available. The project size is a risk factor, not a death sentence, but it is one you should price in.

如果你曾经对小型精品condo情有独钟，觉得它比那些动辄500个单位的大盘更有吸引力，那有一个权衡取舍你值得了解一下。新加坡一项对建筑质量评估的分析发现，较小型condo项目在建筑质量上的评分始终低于较大型项目。这个差距不是偶然的误差，趋势相当稳定，足以说明背后有结构性的原因。

最可能的解释是规模经济。大型项目有条件聘请专职驻场监督，全程专注于质量把关，还能从固定供应商大批量采购材料，在建造的每个环节都有更严格的监管。小型项目往往无法以同样的方式分摊这些成本，于是就会在某些地方打折扣，不一定是出于恶意，只是经济上确实更难做到位。

对买家来说，这是一个实际的提醒，不是叫你完全放弃精品condo。一个口碑良好的发展商推出的小型项目，和新手发展商或有投诉记录的发展商推出的项目，完全是两回事。签约之前，不妨查一查发展商的过往项目，也看看有没有公开的质量评估评分。项目规模是一个风险因素，不是判死刑，但你应该把它纳入考量。
