HDB Resale Prices Have Fallen for Two Quarters in a RowHDB二手房价格已连续两个季度下跌

HDB resale flat prices have now dropped for two straight quarters. That is not a crash, but two consecutive quarterly declines is enough to call it a genuine turn rather than a one-off blip. After years of steady gains that pushed many flat types to record levels, the market has clearly shifted.

A large part of the explanation is the change in buyer behaviour: younger and first-time buyers are increasingly choosing BTO over resale, reducing the pool of active demand. On top of that, a steadier flow of BTO supply gives buyers alternatives that simply did not exist a few years back. Less urgency to buy resale means sellers have less pricing power.

The key question now is whether this is a short correction that stabilises, or the start of a more sustained softening. That will depend on how aggressively HDB keeps releasing new supply, what happens to interest rates, and whether economic conditions tighten further. No one can call that with certainty today.

For sellers, the window of peak prices has passed and the data is now clear on that point. Pricing your flat as if it is still 2023 will mean sitting on the market while better-priced units move around you. For buyers, the mood has shifted enough that you have more room to negotiate than you did at the top. Use it.

HDB二手单位价格已连续两个季度下跌。这不是崩盘，但连续两个季度下跌已足以说明这是一个真实的转折，而不是一时的小波动。在多年来稳步上涨、将许多类型单位推至纪录高位之后，市场显然已经转向。

主要的解释在于买家行为的变化：年轻和首次购房的买家越来越多地选择BTO而非二手，削弱了市场的主动需求。加上更稳定的BTO供应给买家提供了几年前根本不存在的替代选择。购买二手房的迫切感减少，意味着卖家的定价能力减弱。

现在的关键问题是，这究竟是一次短暂调整后将趋于稳定，还是一个更持续性软化的开始？这将取决于HDB持续推出新供应的力度、利率的走势，以及经济环境是否进一步收紧。今天没有人能对此作出确定的判断。

对于卖家，价格高峰的窗口已经过去，数据已经清楚地说明了这一点。若还按2023年的市况来定价，就会坐看周围定价更合理的单位相继成交，而你的单位继续挂在市场上。对于买家，市场情绪已经转变，你现在有比高峰期更大的空间来谈判。好好利用这个机会。
