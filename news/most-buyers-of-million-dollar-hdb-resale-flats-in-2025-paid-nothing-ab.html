Most buyers of million-dollar HDB resale flats in 2025 paid nothing above bank valuation2025年，大多数百万组屋转售买家无需支付COV

$1M+ resales where buyers paid no COV无需支付COV的转售比例

Data from PropNex shows that most buyers who purchased HDB resale flats at $1 million or above in 2025 paid no cash-over-valuation. That means the banks appraised these flats at or very close to what buyers actually paid, with little or no gap between the two figures.

Cash-over-valuation, or COV, is the amount a buyer pays on top of the bank's assessed value, and it must come entirely from cash rather than CPF or a loan. In previous hot markets, COV sometimes ran into tens of thousands of dollars and caught buyers off guard. The fact that it is largely absent at the million-dollar level is genuinely useful information for anyone planning a purchase at that price range.

If you are stretching to buy a high-priced resale flat, this suggests the bank valuation is less likely to come in short and leave you scrambling for extra cash. That said, it is still smart to review recent comparable transactions before making an offer, so you understand what the bank is likely to accept as a reasonable value.

PropNex的数据显示，2025年以$1 million或以上购入HDB转售单位的买家，大多数都没有支付现金超额估价（COV）。也就是说，银行对这些单位的估值，与买家实际支付的价格相当接近，两者之间差距很小。

现金超额估价，也就是COV，指的是买家在银行估值基础上额外支付的金额，这笔钱必须全部用现金支付，不能动用CPF或贷款。在过去楼市火热的时期，COV有时高达数万元，让买家措手不及。百万元级别的单位基本上不需要支付COV，这对于有意在这个价位购房的人来说，是非常实用的信息。

如果你正努力凑够买高价转售单位的钱，这说明银行估值短缺、让你手忙脚乱的情况发生概率较低。话虽如此，在出价之前，还是应该查看近期同类成交记录，了解银行大概会接受的合理估值范围。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
If you are stretching to buy a high-priced resale HDB flat, there is a reasonable chance your bank will value it near the asking price, so you may not need extra cash on top of your loan.如果你打算买高价转售HDB单位，银行估值与开价相近的可能性不低，意味着你或许不需要额外准备一笔现金垫付。
