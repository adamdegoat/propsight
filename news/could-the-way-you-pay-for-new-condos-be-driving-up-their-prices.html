Could The Way You Pay For New Condos Be Driving Up Their Prices?新condo的付款方式，是否正在助推价格走高？

A Business Times analysis has raised a question that many buyers have quietly wondered about: does the progressive payment scheme for new condo launches make it easier for developers to charge more? Under this arrangement, buyers pay in stages as construction progresses rather than settling the full amount upfront, which means the full weight of the price tag is never felt at a single moment.

The argument is straightforward. When a buyer only needs to commit a small percentage today and the rest arrives in tranches over several years, the sticker shock is blunted. Developers can test higher prices with less pushback because the immediate financial hit feels smaller, even when the total sum is very large.

This is worth keeping in mind when you compare new launch prices to resale prices for similar units in the same area. The gap is often striking, and the payment structure is one reason buyers accept price points that would feel unaffordable if the full amount were due on day one. When weighing a new launch against a resale unit, think in terms of the total price and the total interest cost, not just the monthly outlay during construction.

《商业时报》的一篇分析提出了许多买家心中暗自思考的问题：新condo新盘采用的分期付款方案，是否让发展商更容易开出更高的价格？在这套安排下，买家随建筑施工进度分阶段付款，而非一次性付清全款，这意味着价格标签的全部重量从未在某一个时刻同时压下来。

论点很直接。当买家今天只需支付一小部分，其余款项在未来数年内分批到来，价格冲击感就被大大削弱了。发展商可以在阻力较小的情况下试探更高价位，因为眼前的财务压力感觉更轻，尽管总金额其实非常巨大。

当你把同一地区类似单位的新楼发布价与转售价进行比较时，这一点值得牢记。两者之间的差距往往十分明显，而付款结构正是买家接受这些价位的原因之一，如果全款在第一天就要到账，这个价格会让人觉得遥不可及。在衡量新楼盘与转售单位时，请从总价和总利息成本来思考，而不仅仅是施工期间每月的付款额。
