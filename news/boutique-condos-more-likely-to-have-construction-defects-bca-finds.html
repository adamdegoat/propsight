Boutique Condos More Likely to Have Construction Defects, BCA Finds建筑局发现：精品小型Condo的建筑缺陷更多

The Building and Construction Authority has found that smaller boutique condominium projects tend to have more construction defects than larger developments. The reasons cited include less on-site supervision and tighter profit margins for contractors working on smaller jobs, both of which can translate into quality that slips through the cracks.

This is not a minor concern for buyers. Defects discovered after handover can range from cracked tiles and leaking windows to more serious waterproofing or structural problems. On a large development, the developer typically has more resources and reputational incentive to manage build quality. On a smaller boutique project, that oversight can be noticeably thinner.

If you are buying or have recently bought a unit in a small boutique condo, the practical step is to engage a professional inspector before you accept your keys, and to document everything in writing with the developer. Do not assume a premium boutique price tag means premium construction quality, because the BCA data suggests the opposite can be true.

建筑与建设局（BCA）发现，较小规模的精品condo项目往往比大型发展项目存在更多建筑质量缺陷。原因包括现场监工不足，以及承包商在小项目上利润空间偏紧，两者都容易导致质量问题被遗漏。

这对买家来说并非小事。收楼后发现的缺陷，轻则地砖爆裂、窗户漏水，重则防水层或结构性问题。大型发展项目的发展商通常有更多资源，也有更强的声誉动力去把控建造质量；但在较小的精品项目上，这种监督往往明显薄弱。

如果你正在购买或近期已买入小型精品condo的单位，切实可行的做法是：在收取钥匙前聘请专业验屋师进行检查，并将所有问题以书面形式记录，交予发展商跟进。不要以为精品condo的高价标签等同于精品建造质量，因为BCA的数据恰恰显示，情况可能适得其反。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
If you are buying into a small boutique condo, set aside time and budget for a thorough defect inspection before accepting your keys.如果你购买的是小型精品condo，在收取钥匙前务必预留时间和预算，进行全面的缺陷检验。
