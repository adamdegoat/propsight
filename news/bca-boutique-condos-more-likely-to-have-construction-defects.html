BCA: boutique condos more likely to have construction defectsBCA：小型精品condo更容易出现建筑质量问题

Singapore's Building and Construction Authority has published inspection data that should make buyers of smaller condo developments pay closer attention. Its findings show that boutique projects, those with fewer units, tend to record more construction quality defects than larger developments do.

The exact reasons behind the pattern are not spelled out in the BCA data, but plausible factors include thinner site supervision budgets, smaller contractors with less capacity, and fewer internal quality checks compared with a large developer running a 500-unit project. The point is that the gap in defect rates is visible enough for the BCA to flag it publicly.

If you are buying into a boutique condo, do not treat the defect inspection as a formality. Bring in a trained inspector, go through the unit methodically, and document every issue in writing before you accept the keys. Developers are legally required to fix defects raised within the defects liability period, but you have to find and report them first. Build enough time into your schedule for a proper snag-list process and follow-up.

新加坡建设局（BCA）最近公布了一批检查数据，值得正在考虑购买小型condo的买家认真留意。数据显示，精品项目，也就是单位数量较少的那种，出现建筑质量问题的比例，往往高于大型项目。

BCA的数据并没有明确说明背后的原因，但合理的推测包括：小型项目的工地监管预算较少、承包商规模较小、能力有限，以及内部质量把关不如大型发展商那么严格，毕竟后者动辄管理500个单位的项目。关键在于，这个质量差距已经足够明显，让BCA觉得有必要公开点出来。

如果你正在购买精品condo，千万不要把验收检查当成走过场。请一位专业验屋师，仔细逐一检查每个角落，并在领钥匙前把所有问题白纸黑字记录下来。发展商在法律上有责任在保修期内修复所报告的问题，但前提是你得先发现并反映这些问题。给自己留足时间，认认真真走完整个验收和跟进流程。
