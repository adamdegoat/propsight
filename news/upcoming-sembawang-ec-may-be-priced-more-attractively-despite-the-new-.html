10 years new ec resale lock-in periodEC新转售最低持有期

A future executive condo (EC) project in Sembawang is expected to be priced attractively at launch, which would make it stand out in a new launch market where prices have climbed steadily. But buyers need to factor in a major rule change: EC owners must now hold their unit for 10 years before they can sell it on the open market, double the previous five-year requirement.

The logic behind ECs is that you get a private-developer-built home at a lower upfront cost than a full private condo, in exchange for accepting certain restrictions. The old five-year minimum occupation period was already a meaningful commitment. Stretching that to a decade changes the calculation significantly, particularly for buyers who might need to move, upsize or cash out within 10 years.

The entry price can still make compelling sense for the right buyer, but you need to be honest with yourself about your life plans over the next decade. If there is any real chance you will need to sell before that window closes, an EC is a much riskier choice than it used to be.

EC的逻辑在于，你能以低于全私宅condo的价格，入住一个由私人发展商建造的单位，代价是接受若干限制条件。原有的五年最低居住年限已经是一项相当实质的承诺。把这个期限延长至十年，大大改变了整个计算，尤其对那些可能在10年内需要搬迁、换大单位或套现的买家影响尤为明显。

入门价格对合适的买家而言仍然可以非常划算，但你需要对自己未来十年的人生计划诚实以待。如果你真的有可能在这个窗口期结束前需要卖掉单位，EC就比过去危险得多了。
