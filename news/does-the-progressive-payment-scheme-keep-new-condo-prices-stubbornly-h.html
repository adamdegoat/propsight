Does the Progressive Payment Scheme Keep New Condo Prices Stubbornly High?分期付款计划是否让新condo价格居高不下？

When you buy a new launch condo in Singapore, you do not pay the full price upfront. You pay in stages tied to construction milestones, a system called the Progressive Payment Scheme. A Business Times analysis raises a pointed question: does this structure actually insulate developers from any real pressure to cut prices, even when the broader market softens?

The argument makes intuitive sense. Because buyers commit early and make payments slowly over several years, the developer locks in revenue at the original launch price and is not sitting on a pile of completed, depreciating inventory that needs to be moved. A seller of a resale flat or a completed unit feels that pressure acutely. A developer collecting staged payments from buyers who already signed on does not.

This explains something many buyers have noticed but struggle to understand: new launch prices almost never fall after the project opens. Developers may offer stamp duty rebates or furnishing packages, but the headline psf rarely moves down. The financial structure of the scheme removes the incentive to discount.

For buyers thinking about waiting out a new launch for a better deal, this is the reality check. The discount you are waiting for is most likely not coming. If the project suits your needs and the price works today, that is the actual decision in front of you. Waiting for the developer to blink has historically been a losing strategy in Singapore's new launch market.

在新加坡购买新推出的condo，你不需要一次付清全款。你按照建设里程碑分阶段付款，这套制度称为分期付款计划（Progressive Payment Scheme）。《商业时报》的一篇分析提出了一个尖锐的问题：即使整体市场趋于疲软，这种结构是否实际上使发展商完全免受降价压力？

这个论点具有直觉上的说服力。因为买家提前承诺购买并在数年内缓慢付款，发展商以原始开盘价锁定了收入，不必坐拥一堆已竣工、需要急于出清的存货。二手单位或竣工单位的卖家会切实感受到这种压力。而向已签约买家按阶段收取款项的发展商则不然。

这解释了许多买家注意到但难以理解的一件事：新推出的condo价格在项目开盘后几乎从不下跌。发展商或许会提供印花税回扣或家具配套，但每平方英尺的标价几乎不会向下调整。这一付款结构从根本上消除了打折的动机。

对于那些考虑观望新楼盘、等待更好价格的买家，这就是现实所在。你等待的折扣，很可能永远不会来。如果这个项目符合你的需求，而今天的价格也在可接受的范围内，这才是你真正面对的决定。在新加坡的新楼盘市场上，等待发展商先低头，从历史上来看一直是输家的策略。
