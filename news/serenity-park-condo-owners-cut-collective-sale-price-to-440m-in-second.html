Serenity Park Condo Owners Cut Collective Sale Price to $440M in Second AttemptSerenity Park condo业主二度集售，叫价降至$440 million

$440M revised collective sale asking price修订后的集体出售叫价

The owners of Serenity Park, a freehold condominium in the Newton area, have relaunched their collective sale at a reduced asking price of $440 million, after their first attempt attracted no buyers. The lower price is intended to make the deal viable for a developer who needs to factor in construction and redevelopment costs on top of what they pay for the land.

Newton is a prime address and freehold land is genuinely scarce, so the underlying appeal of the site is real. The challenge is that developers have been cautious about large land commitments when new launch prices are not rising fast enough to support aggressive bids. The broader en bloc market has been quiet for some time, not just for this site.

For owners at Serenity Park and anyone at other developments thinking about a collective sale, cutting the price is a necessary step but not a guarantee of a deal. A transaction only happens when a developer is confident it can make money on the project, and that depends on construction costs, future home prices, and competing supply, all of which are outside the sellers' control. Realistic pricing and patience are the only tools on the table.

牛顿是黄金地段，永久地契土地确实稀缺，因此这块地皮本身的吸引力是真实存在的。问题在于，当新推出项目价格涨幅不足以支撑激进出价时，发展商对大规模土地投入一直保持审慎。整个en bloc市场已经沉寂了相当一段时间，不只是这个地段如此。
