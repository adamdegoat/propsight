Gillman Barracks Will Become a Housing Estate with 25 Heritage Buildings and 8 Hectares of Green Space Kept吉门营房将变身公共住宅区，保留25栋历史建筑及8公顷绿地

25 heritage buildings to be retained将保留的历史建筑数目

The government has confirmed that when Gillman Barracks in the Alexandra area is redeveloped into a public housing estate, 25 colonial-era heritage buildings will be conserved alongside 8 hectares of greenery. New HDB flats will be built around these existing structures rather than clearing the entire site. The result will be an estate with a character that is genuinely different from a typical new HDB town.

Gillman Barracks was previously an arts and dining enclave, and before that a British military base, so the site carries a distinct sense of history. Retaining 25 heritage buildings is a meaningful commitment that should give the estate a real sense of place, with mature trees, conserved architecture and open green space rather than the blank-slate feel of a freshly cleared development.

For prospective buyers thinking about this future launch, the heritage and greenery preservation is likely to support a price premium over comparable new HDB projects in less distinctive settings. Estates with strong character and abundant greenery also tend to hold their resale values well over the long term, so buying in at the initial launch could prove to be a sound decision even beyond the first few years of ownership.

政府已确认，吉门营房在亚历山大一带重新发展为公共住宅区时，将保留25栋殖民时期历史建筑，以及8公顷绿地。新的HDB组屋将在这些现有建筑周围兴建，而非将整个地段夷平重建。最终呈现的将是一个与一般新HDB市镇截然不同、别具特色的住宅区。

吉门营房前身是艺文餐饮聚落，再往前追溯则是英国军事基地，因此这片地方本就承载着一段独特的历史。保留25栋历史建筑是一项很有分量的承诺，应能赋予这个住宅区真实的地方感，茂密的成熟树木、经保育的历史建筑和开阔的绿色空间，而非一片刚清空地盘的白板感。

对于有意关注这个未来项目的潜在买家来说，历史保育与绿化保留的承诺，很可能支撑其相对于位置较不特殊地段的同类新HDB项目的价格溢价。具有鲜明特色和丰富绿意的住宅区，长远而言转售价值也往往更为稳固，因此在首次推出时入市，即使着眼最初几年以外的长远表现，也可能是一个明智的决定。
