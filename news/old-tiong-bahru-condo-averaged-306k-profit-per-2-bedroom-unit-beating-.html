Old Tiong Bahru condo averaged $306K profit per 2-bedroom unit, beating newer projects nearby中峇鲁老牌condo每个2房式单位平均获利$306K，超越附近较新项目

$306K average 2-bedder resale profit2房式单位平均转售获利

Resale figures for a long-running condo in Tiong Bahru show it delivered average profits of $306,000 per 2-bedroom unit sold, a figure that beats what owners of newer developments in Bukit Merah and Queenstown made on the same flat type over a comparable period.

The result reflects what many who know the area already understand. Tiong Bahru has a loyal buyer base, a distinct neighbourhood identity, and limited new supply. Those factors keep prices firm even as the buildings age, and they proved more valuable than the newer finishes offered by more recently built projects nearby.

The broader point for anyone comparing investment potential is that age is not a reliable proxy for returns. A well-located older condo in a neighbourhood with genuine demand can outperform a newer launch just down the road. Before discounting an older project because of its age or en-bloc uncertainty, look at what past sellers actually walked away with.

中峇鲁一个历史悠久的condo的转售数字显示，每个已售出的2房式单位平均获利$306,000，在同类比较下，这个数字超过了红山和女皇镇较新发展项目的屋主，在相近期间内从同类单位取得的回报。

这个结果反映了熟悉该区的人早就明白的道理。中峇鲁有一批忠实的买家群体、独特的街区气质，以及有限的新供应量。这些因素让价格在楼龄增长的同时保持坚挺，事实证明，这比附近较新项目提供的现代装潢更有价值。

对于任何在比较投资潜力的人来说，更宽泛的启示在于：楼龄不是判断回报的可靠指标。一个地点优越、需求真实的老牌condo，可能表现胜过隔条街才推出的新项目。在因为楼龄或en bloc的不确定性而否定一个老项目之前，先看看过去的卖家实际上入袋了多少。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
