NUS survey finds Singapore's private condo and HDB resale markets are pulling apartNUS survey finds Singapore's private condo and HDB resale markets are pulling apart

Research from NUS finds that Singapore's private condo market and HDB resale market are increasingly moving to different rhythms, driven by different buyer groups responding to different economic signals. Where the two markets once tracked each other fairly closely, they are now diverging more than before.

Private home buyers tend to be influenced by investment sentiment, wealth effects, and sometimes foreign demand. HDB resale buyers are far more sensitive to local employment conditions, CPF balances, and what they can realistically borrow and repay. As those underlying conditions pull in different directions, the markets follow.

The practical takeaway is to stop thinking about Singapore property as one market that moves together. If condo prices are rising, that does not mean your HDB flat is going up too. If HDB prices are under pressure, that does not automatically signal danger for private homes. Know which market you are in and focus on the indicators that actually affect your segment.
