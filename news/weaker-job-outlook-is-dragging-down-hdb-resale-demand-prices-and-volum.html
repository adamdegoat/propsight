Weaker job outlook is dragging down HDB resale demand, prices and volumesWeaker job outlook is dragging down HDB resale demand, prices and volumes

Analysts are now directly connecting softer employment sentiment to the slide in HDB resale activity. Both transaction volumes and prices fell in Q2 2026, and the job market is being identified as a key reason why demand is pulling back.

This makes sense when you think about who buys HDB resale flats. Most are Singaporeans making a major financial decision that relies heavily on stable income, CPF contributions, and confidence about the future. When job security feels shaky, buyers hesitate or negotiate harder rather than committing.

If you are selling an HDB flat in this environment, price it accurately from day one. Buyers know they have leverage right now and they will use it. Starting high and cutting later costs you time and signals to the market that you were unrealistic to begin with. A well-priced flat is still moving. An overpriced one is sitting.
