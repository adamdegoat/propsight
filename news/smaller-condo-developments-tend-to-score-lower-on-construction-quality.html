Smaller condo developments tend to score lower on construction quality in Singapore新加坡较小型condo项目的建筑质量评分普遍偏低

An analysis of construction quality ratings in Singapore has found that smaller condominium developments consistently score lower than larger ones. The pattern is clear enough that project size appears to be a genuine predictor of quality outcomes, not just a random fluctuation.

The most likely explanation is economics. Rigorous quality control, thorough inspections and skilled site supervision all carry fixed costs. A large development with several hundred units can spread those costs thinly across the project budget. A boutique project with 30 or 50 units faces the same overhead but far fewer units to absorb it, which puts pressure on developers to cut somewhere.

For buyers considering a small or boutique condo, this is not a reason to walk away automatically. It is a reason to do more homework than you might for a larger project. Check what the developer has built before, inspect the showflat finishes carefully, and visit completed projects by the same developer if you can. Seeing how a building has aged after a few years tells you far more than a brochure.

一项对新加坡建筑质量评级的分析发现，较小型的condo项目评分持续低于大型项目。这一规律相当明显，项目规模似乎是预测质量结果的真实指标，而非随机波动。

最可能的解释是经济因素。严格的质量管控、彻底的检查和经验丰富的工地监督都有固定成本。一个拥有数百个单位的大型项目，可以将这些成本薄薄地分摊到庞大的项目预算中。一个只有30或50个单位的精品项目，面临同样的管理成本，却只有少得多的单位来分担，这让发展商不得不在某些方面压缩开支。

对于考虑购买小型或精品condo的买家来说，这并不是打退堂鼓的理由，而是应该比购买大型项目更认真做功课的信号。查看发展商过去建了什么，仔细检查样板间的装修工艺，如果可以的话，亲自参观同一发展商已完成的楼盘。看看一栋楼在几年后的实际状态，比任何楼书都更能说明问题。
