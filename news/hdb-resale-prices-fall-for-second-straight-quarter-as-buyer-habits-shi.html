HDB resale prices fall for second straight quarter as buyer habits shiftHDB resale prices fall for second straight quarter as buyer habits shift

2 quarters straight quarters of hdb price fallsstraight quarters of hdb price falls

HDB resale prices have now fallen for two quarters running, and the slide is not just a slow market working through a soft patch. Analysis of buyer behaviour suggests how people are searching for and choosing flats has genuinely changed, and that shift is doing real work in pushing prices lower.

Two consecutive quarters of price falls puts this beyond a seasonal blip. Whether buyers are taking longer to decide, becoming more selective about flat type, or gravitating toward different estates, the demand pattern has moved in a way that sellers can no longer attribute to bad timing.

If you are listing an HDB flat right now, treat this as a structural signal. The buyers active in today's market have different priorities from those a year or two ago. Pricing your unit based on what a similar flat fetched six months back is likely to leave it sitting unsold for longer than you want. Find out what today's buyers are actually looking for and price accordingly.
