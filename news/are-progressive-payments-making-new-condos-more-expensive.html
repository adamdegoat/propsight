Are progressive payments making new condos more expensive?分期付款方式是否让新condo越来越贵？

Singapore's new launch condos are sold under a progressive payment scheme, where buyers pay a portion of the purchase price at each construction milestone rather than the full sum upfront. A Business Times analysis raises the question of whether this structure actually allows developers to hold prices higher than the market would otherwise bear.

The argument is not complicated. When the full price is spread over three or four years of construction, each individual payment feels manageable. That softened feeling at the point of commitment may reduce the price resistance buyers would otherwise push back with, giving developers room to launch at levels that would feel sharper if paid all at once.

For regular buyers, the practical takeaway is simple: always calculate the total price of a new launch, not just the next stage payment. Then compare that figure directly against what a completed resale unit in the same area actually costs right now, ready to move into. The progressive scheme is not a discount and it is not unique to cheaper projects. It is a payment plan. Keeping that distinction clear helps you make a honest comparison before you commit.

新加坡新推出的condo采用分期付款计划出售，买家在建筑工程每完成一个阶段时支付一部分房价，而不是一次性付清全款。《商业时报》的一篇分析提出了一个问题：这种付款结构是否实际上让发展商得以将价格维持在市场原本无法承受的水平？

道理并不复杂。当全款分摊到三四年的建设周期里，每一笔付款感觉都还可以应付。正是这种在签约时不那么「肉痛」的感觉，可能削弱了买家原本会有的价格抵触，让发展商有空间以更高的价位推盘，如果要一次性付清，买家的感受就会截然不同。

对普通买家来说，实际的启示很简单：买新推盘时，一定要算清楚总价，不要只看下一期的付款金额。然后把这个总价直接拿来跟同区现成的转售单位做比较，看看那些可以马上入住的房子，现在实际卖多少钱。分期付款计划不是折扣，也不是便宜项目的专属安排，它只是一种付款方式。搞清楚这个区别，才能在出手之前做出真正踏实的比较。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
Knowing that the payment structure can obscure the real price helps you make a sharper comparison between a new launch and a completed resale unit.明白付款结构可能模糊真实价格，有助于你在新推盘和现成转售单位之间做出更清醒的比较。
