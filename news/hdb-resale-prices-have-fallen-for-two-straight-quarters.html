HDB Resale Prices Have Fallen for Two Straight QuartersHDB转售价格连续两个季度下滑

HDB resale flat prices have now fallen for two consecutive quarters, a meaningful reversal from the record highs that dominated headlines in recent years. The slide is being driven at least in part by a shift in buyer behaviour that is pulling some demand away from the resale market.

Two quarters of falling prices does not amount to a crash, and anyone holding out for steep discounts is likely to be disappointed. The market is adjusting, not collapsing. Sellers who bought near recent peaks are not panicking, and well-located flats with good remaining lease are still transacting at firm prices.

For buyers, this is a more comfortable environment than it was a year ago. You have a bit more negotiating room and a little less urgency. The practical advice is straightforward: if you find the right flat at a price that fits your finances and timeline, this is a reasonable moment to commit. Waiting for a much sharper fall may mean waiting a very long time.

HDB转售组屋价格已连续两个季度下跌，与近年来频频登上头条的历史高位相比，这是一次明显的逆转。这次下跌至少部分源于买家行为的转变，令部分需求从转售市场流失。

两个季度的价格下滑并不等于崩盘，任何抱着等待大幅折扣心态的人，很可能会失望而归。市场是在调整，而不是在崩溃。在近期高位买入的卖家并没有恐慌，地段好、租约余期充裕的单位依然以稳定的价格成交。

对买家来说，目前的环境比一年前更为舒适。你有稍多一点的议价空间，少了一点非买不可的紧迫感。实际建议也很简单：如果你找到了合适的单位，价格又符合你的财务状况和时间规划，现在是一个相对合理的出手时机。等待价格大幅回落，可能意味着要等上很长一段时间。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
Buyers on the HDB resale market are in a better position than they were a year ago, but prices are easing gradually, not crashing, so do not expect fire-sale deals.目前在HDB转售市场买房的人处于比一年前更有利的位置，但价格是在缓步回落，而非崩盘，因此不要期待会出现大减价的捡漏机会。
