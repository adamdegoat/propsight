Government Auditors Find HDB Gave Flats and Grants to Applicants Who Did Not Qualify审计长办公室发现HDB将组屋及津贴发放给不符合资格的申请者

The Auditor-General's Office, which independently reviews how government agencies manage public money and follow their own rules, found that HDB made processing errors in its flat allocation and housing grant schemes. Some flats were given out and some grants were paid to people who did not actually meet the eligibility criteria. HDB has acknowledged the findings and said it is tightening its verification procedures.

The specific nature of each error has not been spelled out publicly, but the fact that the findings cover both flat allocation and financial grants suggests the checks at multiple stages of the application process were not consistently thorough enough.

If you received an HDB flat or a housing grant in recent years, it is worth satisfying yourself that the information you submitted was accurate and that your eligibility was assessed correctly. HDB has indicated it will review cases, and if an error is later flagged on your application, being prepared and having your documentation in order is far better than being caught off guard.

审计长办公室负责独立审查政府机构如何管理公共资金及遵守自身规定，该办公室发现HDB在组屋分配和住屋津贴计划的审核处理过程中出现了失误。部分组屋被分配给、以及部分津贴被发放给实际上不符合申请资格的人士。HDB已承认相关调查结果，并表示正在加强审核程序。

每项失误的具体情况尚未公开说明，但调查结果同时涉及组屋分配和经济援助津贴，显示申请流程多个阶段的审核工作并不够一贯严谨。

如果你在近年来曾获得HDB组屋或住屋津贴，不妨确认自己当时提交的资料是否准确、资格审核是否正确无误。HDB已表示将审查相关案例，一旦申请中的失误日后被标记出来，事先做好准备、把相关文件整理妥当，总比到时措手不及要好得多。
