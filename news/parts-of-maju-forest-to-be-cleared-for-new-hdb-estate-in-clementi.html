Parts of Maju Forest to Be Cleared for New HDB Estate in Clementi马裕森林部分土地将被清除，为克里门蒂新HDB市镇让路

The government has confirmed that a large part of Maju Forest, a secondary forest in the west, will be cleared to make way for a new public housing estate in Clementi. Some greenery and mature trees will be retained, but most of the land will give way to new HDB blocks.

Clementi is already a well-established estate with strong demand, driven partly by its proximity to the National University of Singapore and good transport links. Adding new HDB supply here is a deliberate move to ease price pressure in the west over the longer term.

For buyers watching this part of Singapore, more supply over the coming years should mean less upward pressure on resale prices in the area. For existing residents and nature lovers, the trade-off is real: the forest that stands there today will largely be gone, and the neighbourhood will feel meaningfully different once new blocks are occupied.

政府已确认，位于西部的次生林马裕森林，大部分土地将被清除，以在克里门蒂兴建新的公共住屋市镇。部分绿化带和成熟树木将予以保留，但大多数土地将让路给新的HDB组屋楼群。

克里门蒂本就是一个配套成熟、需求旺盛的市镇，毗邻新加坡国立大学，交通连接也十分便利。在这里增加新的HDB供应，是政府刻意为之、旨在长远缓解西部价格压力的举措。

对关注这一带楼市的买家来说，未来几年的新供应增加，应能减轻该区二手市场的上涨压力。但对于现有居民和热爱自然的人而言，这个代价是真实存在的：如今矗立于此的森林将基本消失，待新组屋入住后，整个社区的面貌也将大为改变。
