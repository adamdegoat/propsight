Queenstown 2-room HDB flat sells for $696,000, a new national recordQueenstown一间2房式HDB单位以$696,000成交，创全国新纪录

$696,000 national record 2-room hdb price全国2房式HDB最高成交价纪录

A 2-room HDB flat in Queenstown has sold for $696,000, setting a new national record for the smallest standard flat type in the public housing system. The price is far above what most 2-room flats trade for across Singapore, where the vast majority change hands well below $300,000.

The premium comes down to location. Queenstown is a city-fringe estate with strong connectivity, established amenities and consistent demand from buyers who want proximity to the central business district without paying private property prices. Buyers were clearly willing to pay a steep premium to secure a flat in this part of town, even at just 2 rooms.

The record is another data point confirming what Singapore property watchers already know: location drives value above almost everything else. A flat's size or age matters far less than where it sits. For sellers in similarly well-located estates, this sets a new benchmark. For buyers, it is a prompt to think carefully about what you are actually paying for when you stretch for a name address.

这高出市价的溢价，归根结底还是地段使然。Queenstown地处城市边缘，交通便利，配套成熟，一直以来都深受那些希望靠近中央商业区、又不想付私宅价格的买家追捧。显然，买家愿意为这一带的单位付出不小的溢价，哪怕只是一间2房式。

这项纪录再次印证了新加坡房产界早已心知肚明的道理：地段，几乎凌驾于一切之上。一间单位的大小或楼龄，远没有它的所在位置来得重要。对于同样身处黄金地段的卖家来说，这无疑树立了新的参考标杆。对买家而言，则是一个提醒：当你为了一个好地址而大幅拉高预算时，不妨仔细想清楚，你究竟在为什么买单。
