Bayshore Drive site draws record $2.13B bid, the highest ever for a non-CBD government land plotBayshore Drive地段收获$2.13B竞标价，创非CBD政府售地历史最高纪录

$2.13B record non-CBD government land bid非CBD政府售地最高竞标价

A consortium led by Frasers Property won the government tender for a mixed-use site on Bayshore Drive with a bid of $2.13 billion, the highest price ever paid for a government land sale plot outside the central business district. The site sits near the upcoming Bayshore MRT station in the East Coast area and is expected to be developed into a mix of homes and commercial space.

At $2.13 billion for the land alone, the numbers set a clear floor for what the developer needs to recover. Construction costs, financing and margin all sit on top of that. Record land prices like this almost always translate into above-average launch prices when the units eventually go on sale to the public.

If you are planning to buy in the Bayshore area, use this as a reference point when weighing new launches against older resale options nearby. The homes coming from this site are unlikely to be priced modestly. For existing owners in the East Coast corridor, a flagship development of this scale and price tends to support broader prices in the neighbourhood over time.

由Frasers Property牵头的财团以$2.13 billion的竞标价，赢得了Bayshore Drive综合发展地段的政府招标，创下中央商业区以外政府售地的历史最高价。该地段毗邻即将开通的Bayshore MRT站，位于东海岸一带，预计将发展为住宅与商业空间混合的项目。

单是地价已达$2.13 billion，这个数字为发展商需要回收的成本设定了一个清晰的底线。建筑费用、融资成本和利润都叠加在这之上。这样的破纪录地价，几乎必然会转化为单位上市时高于平均水平的售价。

如果你有意在Bayshore一带购房，在权衡新推项目与附近二手房选项时，可以把这个数字作为参考。从这个地段推出的新房，价格不太可能会便宜。对于东海岸一带的现有屋主来说，这种规模和价位的旗舰发展项目，往往能在长期内支撑整个区域的房价。
