$5.5M home caught in inheritance dispute住宅陷入遗产纠纷

A property sale worth $5.5 million in Singapore ran into serious legal trouble when the seller's estate became caught up in an inheritance dispute. The buyer found themselves unable to complete the transaction cleanly and faced complications that are costly and time-consuming to resolve at any price point.

When an estate is disputed, who legally has the right to sell the property and on what terms may be unresolved until a court rules. A buyer who has already paid a deposit or signed an option to purchase can find themselves in a difficult position: the sale cannot proceed normally, but extracting their money or enforcing the deal may require its own legal battle.

This is the exact risk that proper conveyancing is designed to catch before any money moves. A conveyancing lawyer's title search is supposed to confirm that the seller has clear and undisputed ownership, that there are no outstanding caveats or claims on the property, and that the buyer will receive clean title on completion. On a purchase of this size, or any size, cutting corners on conveyancing to save a few hundred dollars is not a trade-off worth considering.

新加坡一宗价值$5.5 million的房产买卖遭遇严重法律麻烦，卖方的遗产陷入了继承纠纷。买家无法顺利完成交易，面临的种种麻烦既费时又费钱，无论房价高低，都是一场难以收拾的局面。

当遗产存在争议时，谁有合法权利出售该房产、以什么条件出售，往往要等到法院裁决才能厘清。已经付了订金或签了购房意向书（option to purchase）的买家，会陷入两难：买卖无法正常推进，但要拿回钱或强制执行合约，自己又可能得另打一场官司。

正规的过户程序，正是为了在金钱易手之前发现这类风险。过户律师进行的产权查册，就是要确认卖方拥有清晰、无争议的业权，房产上没有未解除的产权声明（caveat）或任何索偿，买家在完成交易后能取得干净的产权。无论是这个价位的房产，还是任何其他价位，为了省几百块而在过户手续上走捷径，都不是一个值得考虑的取舍。
