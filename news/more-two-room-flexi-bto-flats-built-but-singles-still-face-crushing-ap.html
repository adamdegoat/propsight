More Two-Room Flexi BTO Flats Built, But Singles Still Face Crushing Application RatesHDB两房灵活式BTO单位供应增加，但单身申请竞争依旧惨烈

HDB has been adding more two-room flexi flats to address demand from singles, who are among the few groups eligible to apply for BTO flats on their own. Despite the higher supply numbers, application rates for these units remain extremely high, meaning the queue is still very long for each available flat.

Part of the reason demand refuses to ease is that buyer behaviour has not shifted much. Singles are still clustering their applications around the same popular towns, so a larger total supply does not translate into shorter queues in the areas people actually want to live in.

For singles in the BTO queue right now, the honest takeaway is this: more flats being built does not automatically improve your chances. If you keep applying to the same high-demand towns as everyone else, your odds stay poor. Spreading your options to less popular towns, or applying across multiple launches, will do more for your chances than waiting for supply to catch up with demand.

HDB一直在增加两房灵活式组屋的供应，以满足单身人士的需求，单身人士是少数几个可以独立申请BTO的群体之一。尽管供应数字有所提升，这类单位的申请率依然极高，意味着每个可用单位的等候队伍仍然很长。

需求迟迟未能缓解，部分原因在于买家行为并没有太大改变。单身申请者还是集中在同一批热门市镇，因此总供应量虽然提高，人们真正想住的地区却并未因此缩短排队。

对于目前正在BTO队伍中等待的单身人士，说实话的结论是：多建几个单位，不代表你的机会自动变大。如果你一直和其他人一样申请同样的热门市镇，中签率还是会很低。把选择拓展到较冷门的市镇，或跨越多个销售推介期申请，对提高胜算来说，远比单纯等待供应追上需求更实际。
