People's Park Centre Launches Third En Bloc Attempt at a Lower $1.48 Billion牛车水人民公园中心第三度尝试en bloc，指导价降至$1.48 billion

$1.48B third en bloc guide price第三次en bloc指导价

People's Park Centre in Chinatown has launched its third attempt at a collective sale, this time with a guide price of $1.48 billion. That is $320 million less than the previous asking price, which failed to attract a buyer. The ageing mixed-use development has been trying to find a developer willing to redevelop it for several years.

Cutting hundreds of millions off the asking price shows how seriously the owners want a deal. But two failed attempts are a signal worth taking seriously. Developers weigh the land cost against what they can realistically sell the redeveloped units for, and the numbers have not stacked up on two separate occasions already.

Owners at People's Park Centre who are counting on an en bloc payout should stay realistic. A lower price helps, but it is not a guarantee. Even at $1.48 billion, a developer has to believe they can make the numbers work in the current market. Owners should not put their financial plans on hold for a deal that may not materialise.

牛车水的人民公园中心正式发起第三次集体出售，这次的指导价定在$1.48 billion，比上一次没能吸引到买家的叫价少了$320 million。这个年岁渐长的综合用途发展项目，已经寻寻觅觅好几年，一直在等一个愿意重建的发展商。

叫价砍掉几亿元，可见业主们有多迫切想成交。但两次失败是个值得认真看待的信号。发展商会把地价拿来跟重建后单位的实际售价相比，而这笔账在两次机会里都没能算得过来。

人民公园中心里指望en bloc分钱的业主，还是要保持现实的心态。降价固然有帮助，但并不是保证。就算是$1.48 billion这个价位，发展商也要相信在目前市场上这门生意行得通，才会出手。业主不应该把自己的财务计划押在一个可能落空的交易上。
