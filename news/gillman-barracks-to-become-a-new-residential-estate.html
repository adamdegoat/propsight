Gillman Barracks to Become a New Residential Estate吉曼军营将变身全新住宅区

25 heritage buildings to be retained将保留的历史建筑数量

Gillman Barracks, the former colonial military camp in southern Singapore near Labrador, has been earmarked to become a new residential estate. The government plans to build homes on the site while keeping 25 heritage buildings and the existing greenery intact.

The site sits close to the Southern Ridges and has more recently been used as an arts cluster. Retaining 25 heritage structures is a genuine commitment in a city that often clears land completely before rebuilding, which suggests this estate will have real character rather than feeling like a blank slate development dropped into the landscape.

Buyers who are drawn to quiet, green parts of the south should start paying attention now. Planning details will take time to firm up, but once land parcels hit the market or BTO launches are announced for the area, demand will move quickly. Getting familiar with the location early puts you in a better position when the time comes.
