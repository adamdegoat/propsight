Condo VIP sales under scrutiny as agents get first pick of new unitsCondo新盘VIP预售遭调查，经纪人抢先挑走优质单位

Authorities in Singapore are scrutinising how some property agents managed to buy units at new condo launches ahead of the general public. The VIP preview system is meant to allow certain categories of buyers early access, but it appears that a number of agents used those slots to secure units for their own personal purchases before ordinary buyers could enter the queue.

Getting first pick at a popular launch is not a minor perk. The best-facing units, the higher floors, and the layouts with the strongest resale track record tend to go in the first hours. If agents are using insider access to grab those units for themselves, regular buyers are left choosing from what remains at the same or higher prices with no transparency about why the best options are already gone.

If regulations tighten around who qualifies for VIP access and how those allocations can be used, it removes one quiet structural disadvantage that has worked against buyers purchasing for their own homes. A level queue on launch day is a fairer outcome and the scrutiny now being applied is a step in that direction.

新加坡当局目前正在调查，部分房产经纪人是如何在新condo开盘时，赶在普通公众之前购入单位的。VIP预览制度本是为特定类别的买家提供提前入场的机会，但看来有一些经纪人利用这些名额，在普通买家还没进入排队通道之前，就替自己抢先买下了心仪的单位。

在热门新盘中抢先挑选，可不是件小事。朝向最好的单位、高楼层，以及转售表现最出色的户型，往往在开盘头几个小时就被一扫而空。如果经纪人借助内部渠道替自己抢走这些单位，普通买家只能从剩下的里头挑，价格却一分不少，而且根本不知道为什么最好的选择早就没了。

如果当局收紧VIP通道的资格审核，以及相关名额的使用方式，就能消除一个长期以来悄悄压低自住买家机会的结构性不公平。开盘当天人人机会均等，才是更公平的结果，而眼下展开的调查，正是朝这个方向迈出的一步。
