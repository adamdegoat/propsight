Bought this 2,800 sq ft condo for its rental income, but a big change is coming. Sell or hold?当初买这个2,800 sq ft的condo是为了租金收入，但一个大变化要来了——卖还是继续持有？

A couple bought a 2,800 sq ft condo unit specifically because it generated strong rental income. Now something material about the property is set to change, and they are facing a decision that many investment property owners eventually encounter: cut and run, or stay the course.

The decision gets worked through with actual numbers, weighing the rental yield currently earned against what they might net if they sold now versus after the change takes effect. The math shifts depending on how much the change affects either the rent they can charge or the price a future buyer would pay for the unit.

The broader lesson applies to any condo owner holding a unit for yield. When circumstances shift, the instinct is often to hold on and hope things stabilise. The smarter move is to run the numbers honestly rather than emotionally. Whether to sell or hold depends on what the rental income actually looks like after the change, and what the market will pay for the unit today compared to later.

一对夫妇买入一个2,800 sq ft的condo单位，看中的就是它能带来稳定的租金收入。如今，这个物业有一些实质性的变化即将发生，他们面临着许多投资房产的业主迟早都会遇到的抉择：趁早套现离场，还是继续持有。

这个决定结合了实际数字来分析，把目前赚取的租金收益、现在卖出的净所得，以及等变化生效后再卖的净所得，放在一起权衡。数学上的答案，会随着这个变化对他们能收取的租金或未来买家愿意出的价格影响有多大，而出现不同的结论。

更大的道理适用于所有持有condo用于收益的业主：当情况发生变化，本能反应往往是继续守着、希望市场会稳定下来。更聪明的做法是老老实实地算数字，而不是凭感情做决定。卖还是守，取决于变化发生后的租金实际收益，以及市场现在和以后愿意为这个单位付出的价格。
