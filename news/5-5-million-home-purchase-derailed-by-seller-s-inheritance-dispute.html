$5.5M home caught in inheritance dispute房产遭遗产纠纷牵连

A buyer who agreed to purchase a $5.5 million Singapore property saw the entire deal fall apart when it emerged that the property was caught up in a family inheritance dispute involving the seller's estate. From the outside, the transaction looked clean. Underneath, the seller could not deliver clear title.

Property transactions in Singapore are generally well-regulated, but complications around estates, probate, and disputed ownership can lurk below the surface of what appears to be a straightforward sale. A caveat or a pending court matter may not be visible at all unless someone goes looking for it properly.

The lesson is simple and it applies at every price point, not just $5.5 million: get a lawyer to run a proper title search before you sign the Option to Purchase. A conveyancing lawyer will verify that the seller has unencumbered title and check for caveats, charges, or disputes that could derail the deal. The legal fees are a small price compared to discovering a problem after you have already committed.

一名买家同意购入一套价值$5.5 million的新加坡房产，整笔交易却在发现该房产牵涉卖家遗产家庭纠纷后宣告瓦解。从外表来看，这笔交易干干净净。但表面之下，卖家根本无法提供清晰的产权。

新加坡的房产交易整体受到严格监管，但遗产、遗嘱认证及产权争议方面的纠葛，往往潜伏在一笔表面上看似简单直接的买卖之下。如果没有人专门去查，一份产权警示或一宗待决的法庭事项，可能根本不会显现出来。

这其中的教训简单明了，而且适用于任何价位，不仅仅是$5.5 million：在签署购房意向书（Option to Purchase）之前，委托律师进行正式的产权审查。产权转让律师会核实卖家是否拥有无负担产权，并检查可能导致交易破裂的产权警示、抵押或纠纷。与事后才发现问题的代价相比，律师费实在微不足道。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
