Private and HDB resale markets are going their separate ways, NUS survey findsNUS调查发现，私宅与HDB转售市场走势渐行渐远

A National University of Singapore survey has found that Singapore's private condo market and its HDB resale market are now being pushed along by increasingly distinct groups of buyers. The financial profiles, motivations and sensitivities of these two groups have diverged enough that treating the two markets as parts of one connected whole is becoming misleading.

Private condo buyers tend to have higher household incomes, may include permanent residents and foreigners, and carry larger mortgages that make them more sensitive to interest rate changes. HDB resale buyers are predominantly Singapore citizens relying on CPF savings and housing grants, with affordability tied closely to employment conditions and grant structures. Different levers are moving each market.

The practical implication is simple: what is happening to condo prices right now tells you very little about what your HDB flat is worth, and vice versa. If you own or are buying in one of these segments, pay attention to data from that market specifically rather than reading across from the other.

新加坡国立大学（NUS）的一项调查发现，新加坡私宅condo市场和HDB转售市场，如今已受到截然不同的买家群体所推动。这两个群体在财务状况、购买动机和敏感因素上的差异，已经大到让人把两个市场视为一个相互关联整体的看法变得具有误导性。

私宅condo买家通常家庭收入更高，可能包括永久居民和外籍人士，贷款额也更大，因此对利率变动更为敏感。HDB转售买家则以新加坡公民为主，依赖CPF储蓄和购房补贴，购买力与就业状况和补贴结构紧密挂钩。驱动两个市场的杠杆各不相同。

实际意义很简单：condo价格目前的走势，对于判断你的HDB组屋值多少钱，几乎没有参考价值，反之亦然。如果你持有或正在购买其中一个市场的房产，就应该专门关注那个市场的数据，而不是从另一个市场的动态来推断。
