Bought a Large Condo for Rental Income, But the Story Is Changing: Should We Sell?买了一套大condo收租，但情况正在改变：该不该卖？

2,800 sqft condo size in rental dilemma租赁困境中的condo面积

A condo owner is facing a dilemma that many Singapore property investors will recognise. They bought a large 2,800 square foot unit specifically to generate rental income, but the conditions that made that strategy attractive have since shifted. Rents have softened from their peak, larger units take longer to lease, and the monthly numbers no longer look as comfortable as they once did.

Holding an investment property can feel like the cautious choice, but it carries real costs that do not pause while you wait. Mortgage interest, maintenance fees, property tax, and periods of vacancy all eat into returns. If the rental income no longer adequately covers those costs, staying put is itself a financial decision with consequences, not simply a neutral default.

The more useful question is not whether the property has gone up in value since you bought it, but whether the original reason for buying it still holds. If you bought for rental yield and the yield has materially weakened, that is worth an honest reassessment rather than hoping conditions improve on their own.

Timing a sale is genuinely difficult and there is no perfect moment. But holding too long because selling feels like admitting a mistake tends to produce worse outcomes than reviewing the position clearly and acting on what the current numbers actually say.

一位condo业主面临着许多新加坡房产投资者都会感同身受的两难处境。他们当初以出租为目的，买下了一套2,800平方英尺的大型单位，但使这个策略具有吸引力的条件已经改变。租金从高峰期回落，大型单位需要更长时间才能租出去，每月的数字已不像从前那样令人安心。

持有投资房产可能让人感觉是谨慎的选择，但它带来的实际成本并不会因为你在等待而暂停。贷款利息、管理费、房产税以及空置期，都在持续蚕食回报。如果租金收入已经无法充分覆盖这些成本，继续持有本身就是一个有后果的财务决定，而不仅仅是一个中性的默认选项。

更有用的问题，不是房产自你买入以来是否升值了，而是当初买它的理由是否仍然成立。如果你买的目的是租金收益，而租金收益已经明显下滑，这值得诚实地重新评估，而不是寄望于情况自己好转。

把握卖出时机确实很难，从来没有所谓完美的时机。但因为卖出感觉像是承认犯了错而迟迟不动，往往会比清醒地审视现状、根据当前数字采取行动，带来更差的结果。
