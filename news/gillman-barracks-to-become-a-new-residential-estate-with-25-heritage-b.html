Gillman Barracks to become a new residential estate, with 25 heritage buildings and 8ha of greenery keptGillman Barracks将发展成新住宅区，25座历史建筑与8公顷绿地获保留

8ha greenery preserved at GillmanGillman保留绿地面积

The former British military camp at Gillman Barracks in Alexandra will be redeveloped into a new residential estate. The government plans to retain 25 heritage buildings within the site and preserve around 8 hectares of greenery, so the new estate will blend housing with the area's colonial-era structures and existing tree canopy.

Gillman Barracks has spent recent years operating as an arts and gallery cluster, so the shift to residential use is a significant change of character. Keeping the heritage buildings and green space intact suggests the new precinct will have a distinct identity, the kind of thing that tends to appeal to buyers who want somewhere that feels different from a standard new housing development.

For property owners and buyers in the Alexandra, Labrador Park and Telok Blangah corridor, more housing supply is coming to the area. Additional supply generally eases price pressure on surrounding developments, though the heritage and greenery angle could attract enough demand from across Singapore to keep the wider neighbourhood buoyant.

位于亚历山大的前英军营地Gillman Barracks将重新发展为新住宅区。政府计划保留园区内25座历史建筑，并保存约8公顷的绿色空间，因此新住宅区将融合住房、殖民地时期建筑遗迹与现有的树冠绿地，形成独特的社区面貌。

Gillman Barracks近年来作为艺术与画廊集群运营，转型为住宅用途是一个重大的性质转变。保留历史建筑和绿地的计划显示，新住宅区将拥有鲜明的个性气质，这类特色往往吸引那些希望居住地与普通新住宅项目有所不同的买家。

对于亚历山大、Labrador Park和直落布兰雅走廊一带的业主和买家来说，更多住房供应即将进入该区域。额外供应通常会减轻周边楼盘的价格升幅压力，不过历史建筑和绿化景观的特色，或许能从全新加坡吸引足够的需求，支撑整个周边地区的房市。
