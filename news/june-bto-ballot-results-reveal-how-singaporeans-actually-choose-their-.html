June BTO ballot results reveal how Singaporeans actually choose their flats六月BTO抽签结果，揭示新加坡人挑选组屋的真实偏好

A breakdown of June 2026 BTO application patterns reveals clear trends in how Singaporeans decide which projects to ballot for. Location, flat type and whether a town is classified as mature or non-mature are among the strongest factors determining whether a project ends up heavily oversubscribed or relatively lightly contested.

Mature estate projects in central or well-connected parts of Singapore tend to attract far more applicants per available flat, which means your odds of a successful ballot are substantially lower. Non-mature town projects, or those in areas that are still developing, typically see less competition, giving applicants with more flexibility a meaningful advantage.

If you are planning a BTO application and can afford to be flexible on location or flat size, understanding where demand is thinner gives you a real strategic edge. The June data provides a live snapshot of what other applicants are actively chasing and, just as usefully, what they are willing to pass on.

位于中部或交通便捷地区的成熟市镇项目，每个单位往往吸引远更多的申请者，这意味着你成功抽中的概率大幅降低。非成熟市镇的项目，或位于仍在发展中地区的项目，竞争通常较少，给予选择上较灵活的申请者一个实质性的优势。
