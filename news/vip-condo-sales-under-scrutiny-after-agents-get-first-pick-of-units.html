VIP condo sales under scrutiny after agents get first pick of units代理优先选购单位引关注，新condo推售VIP程序受到审查

Regulators are now looking at how developers and agencies manage VIP access at new condo launches. The specific concern is that agents were getting first pick of units before ordinary members of the public could even see what was available. That meant the best floors, the better-facing stacks, and sometimes the lower-priced units were already spoken for before the public queue opened.

This practice has been an open secret in the industry for a long time. Agents bring buyer clients, so developers give them early access as an incentive. The problem is that it creates a two-tier system where buyers with agent connections start the race ahead of everyone else. For someone who did their research and showed up on launch day without an agent, the best options may already be gone.

If regulators follow through with tighter rules, it could mean a genuine ballot process where every buyer starts from the same position. That would be a meaningful change for the buyers who have felt frozen out of good units at competitive launches.

监管机构目前正在审查发展商与代理公司如何管理新condo推售的VIP通道。具体问题在于，代理在普通公众还未能查看房源时，已经优先挑选单位。这意味着最高楼层、朝向较佳的单位，有时还有售价较低的单位，在公众排队购买之前就已经被预订了。

这种做法在业内早已是公开的秘密。代理带来买家客户，发展商便以提前入场作为回报。问题在于，这形成了一个两级制度，有代理关系的买家比所有人抢先一步。对于那些做足功课、在推售日独自现身却没有代理的买家来说，最好的选择可能早已不见了。

若监管机构落实更严格的规定，可能会推行真正的抽签制度，让每位买家从同一起跑线出发。对于那些在热门推售中感觉被排除在外的买家而言，这将是一个实质性的改变。
