Bayshore Drive Site Sells for Record $2.13 Billion, the Highest Ever for a Non-City-Centre PlotBayshore Drive地段以$2.13 billion标得，创非市区住宅地段最高价纪录

$2.13b winning bid for bayshore siteBayshore地段中标价

A Frasers Property-led consortium paid $2.13 billion for a government land site at Bayshore Drive, making it the highest price ever paid for a residential site outside Singapore's city centre. The plot is mixed-use, meaning it will eventually include both condos and commercial space. It sits close to the new Bayshore MRT station, which gives it strong connectivity credentials.

To put that land cost in perspective: developers need to recover every dollar of that bid through the unit prices they charge buyers. The land cost alone sets a high floor for what finished units must sell at just to break even, before construction, financing and profit margins are added on top.

If you are drawn to the Bayshore area, buying into an existing development there now, or watching this project's early launch prices closely, is likely to be cheaper than waiting for later phases once the market absorbs the full weight of this record bid. Record land prices tend to pull surrounding values upward over time, so early movers in the area tend to benefit most.

由Frasers Property牵头的财团以$2.13 billion竞得Bayshore Drive一幅政府售地，成为新加坡市区以外有史以来成交价最高的住宅地段。这幅地是综合用途地段，未来将同时建有condo和商业空间，地段毗邻新的Bayshore MRT站，交通连接条件相当优越。

要理解这个地价的意义：开发商需要通过向买家收取的单位售价来回收这笔竞地款项的每一分钱。光是地价本身，就已经为竣工单位的售价设定了一个很高的下限，这还未算上建筑成本、融资成本和利润空间。

如果你有意在Bayshore一带置业，现在就入手该区现有发展项目，或者密切留意这个新项目早期推出的价格，很可能会比等到市场消化了这个创纪录竞地价之后再出手更加合算。创纪录的地价往往会带动周边楼价一起上扬，所以越早在这个区域布局，往往越能受益。
