Younger Buyers Are Pulling Back From HDB Resale as BTO Gets More Attractive年轻买家减少购买HDB二手单位，BTO吸引力持续上升

Data from Realion shows that younger buyers are making up a smaller share of HDB resale transactions. The shift is being driven by the BTO system becoming a more attractive option for first-timers, with shorter waiting times and better grant structures making new flats the preferred route for many young couples who might previously have gone straight to resale.

Two or three years ago, BTO waiting times were running close to five years in some cases, and many couples skipped the queue entirely and paid up for resale just to get into a home sooner. That calculation has changed. HDB has added more flats with shorter completion timelines, and the wait is less painful than it used to be.

For resale sellers targeting first-time buyer couples, this is worth paying attention to. The buyer pool has not vanished, but it has gotten more selective. Flats that qualify for maximum grants, are in good condition, and are priced honestly will still transact. Those relying purely on location or a perceived premium may find fewer takers willing to stretch.

If you are selling an HDB flat and banking on young couples as your likely buyers, the environment has shifted under your feet. Pricing realistically and making your flat easy to qualify for grants will matter more now than it did at the height of the resale boom.

Realion的数据显示，年轻买家在HDB二手交易中所占的比例正在下降。这一转变的推手，是BTO系统对于首次购房者而言变得更具吸引力，等候时间缩短，津贴结构改善，使得全新BTO单位成为许多年轻夫妇的首选，而这些人此前可能会直接转向二手市场。

两三年前，BTO的等候时间在某些情况下接近五年，许多夫妇干脆跳过排队，多花一些钱买二手单位，只为尽快入住。这个盘算已经改变。HDB增加了更多竣工时间较短的单位，等候期已没有以前那么漫长。

对于以首次购房夫妇为目标买家的二手单位卖家，这一点值得关注。买家群体并未消失，但变得更加挑剔。能申请最高津贴、状况良好、定价实际的单位仍然会成交。那些纯粹依赖地段或某种溢价优势的单位，可能会发现愿意出高价的买家越来越少。

如果你正在出售HDB单位，并寄望于年轻夫妇作为主要买家，你所处的市场环境已经悄悄改变。实际定价，并确保你的单位容易符合津贴申请资格，现在比二手市场高峰期更为重要。

What it means for you对你意味着什么
Resale HDB sellers may find their pool of willing buyers narrowing, particularly for older flats or those with less grant eligibility.出售HDB二手单位的屋主可能发现潜在买家群体在缩减，尤其是对于较旧的单位或能申请津贴较少的单位。
