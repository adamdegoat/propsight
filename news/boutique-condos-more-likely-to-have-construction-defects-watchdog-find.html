Boutique Condos More Likely To Have Construction Defects, Watchdog Finds监管机构发现：精品condo更容易出现建筑质量缺陷

Singapore's Building and Construction Authority has found that smaller boutique condo developments tend to rack up more quality defects than larger projects. The finding is not entirely surprising. Smaller developers often work with tighter budgets, leaner teams, and less oversight, and that tends to show up in the finished product.

A boutique condo can appeal because of its exclusivity, lower density, and sometimes its location. But none of that guarantees good workmanship. Cracked tiles, faulty fittings, water seepage, and poor finishing are among the defects that show up at handover, and the BCA data suggests they appear more often in these smaller projects.

If you are buying a boutique condo, treat your defect inspection as seriously as any other stage of the purchase. Hire a professional inspector if you can, go through every room methodically, and document everything in writing before you hand over the final payment. The developer is legally obligated to fix defects raised during the defect liability period, but that only protects you if you actually raise them.

新加坡建设局发现，规模较小的精品condo发展项目往往比大型项目积累更多质量缺陷。这一发现并不令人完全意外。小型发展商通常预算更紧、团队更精简、监督也较少，这些往往都会在最终成品上有所体现。

精品condo的吸引力可能在于其独特性、较低密度，有时还有得天独厚的地理位置。但这些都不能保证施工质量。瓷砖开裂、配件故障、渗水漏水、收尾粗糙，都是验房时常见的缺陷，而建设局的数据显示，这些问题在小型项目中出现的频率更高。

如果你正在购买精品condo，请把验房环节当作整个购买过程中同等重要的阶段。如有可能，聘请专业验房师，逐房逐室有条不紊地检查，并在交付尾款前把所有问题书面记录在案。发展商在质量保证期内有法律义务修复你提出的缺陷，但这份保障的前提是你确实把问题提了出来。
