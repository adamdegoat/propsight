Most Buyers of Million-Dollar HDB Flats Paid No Cash Premium in 20252025年大多数百万元HDB组屋买家无需支付现金溢价

Data from PropNex covering 2025 shows that the majority of HDB resale flats that sold above $1 million did not require buyers to pay any cash over valuation. This means official HDB valuations for these high-priced units broadly kept pace with what buyers actually paid, allowing most of the purchase to be funded through CPF and home loans rather than extra cash out of pocket.

Cash over valuation was a significant pain point in earlier property cycles when valuations lagged the market and buyers had to produce cash top-ups that could not come from CPF or bank loans. The current data suggests that at the high end of the resale market, at least, valuations are tracking transaction prices more accurately.

That said, this is a market-level trend, not a guarantee for any individual deal. Valuations vary by unit and can shift quickly. If you are making an offer on an expensive resale flat, always get your own valuation done before committing. Knowing that number in advance tells you exactly what financing you can use and removes an unpleasant surprise at the final stage.

PropNex的2025年数据显示，以逾$1 million成交的HDB转售组屋中，大多数买家无需支付任何超出估价的现金（cash over valuation）。这意味着这些高价单位的官方HDB估价，基本上与买家实际支付的金额相符，大部分购房款项都可以通过CPF和房贷支付，无需自掏腰包补差价。

在早前的房产周期里，现金溢价曾让买家叫苦不迭，当时估价跟不上市场，买家不得不自行补上一笔无法动用CPF或银行贷款的现金差额。目前的数据显示，至少在转售市场的高端部分，估价已更紧贴成交价格。

话虽如此，这只是市场层面的趋势，并不代表每宗交易都会如此。估价因单位而异，也可以变动很快。如果你正在出价购买高价转售组屋，务必在确认购买前先自行完成估价。提前掌握这个数字，就能清楚知道自己可以动用哪些资金，避免在最后关头出现意外。
