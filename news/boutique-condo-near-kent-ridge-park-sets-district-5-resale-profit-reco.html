A unit in a boutique condo near Kent Ridge Park in western Singapore has just set a new resale profit record for District 5, clocking a gain of $5.4 million. That is the highest profit ever recorded for a condo resale in a district that covers West Coast, one-north and the surrounding area.

The combination of factors behind this result is worth understanding. Small developments with few units have less internal competition when sellers come to market. An address near the park and within easy reach of the one-north research and business hub adds genuine appeal that holds up over time. And holding for long enough in a well-located project can compound those advantages into a very large absolute gain.

For owners in the West Coast and one-north corridor, this is a useful reference point. It does not mean every condo in the area will produce the same outcome, but it confirms that scarcity and location can drive results well above what a typical large-scale development delivers. If you have been sitting on a unit in a small project in this part of Singapore and wondering whether the timing is right to sell, this record is a sign that demand at the top of the market remains very real.
