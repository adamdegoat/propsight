The Tan Boon Liat Building along Outram Road, long known as a hub for furniture and interior design businesses, has changed hands through a collective sale for $950 million. What makes this site stand out is that it is freehold, which is genuinely rare for a central Singapore location of this size.

Freehold land near Outram Park MRT, within reach of the Greater Southern Waterfront corridor and the Tanjong Pagar area, is exactly the kind of asset developers pay a premium to secure. The expectation is that the site will be redeveloped into a premium residential project, possibly with some retail or commercial space. At $950 million for the land alone, the finished homes will carry a significant price tag.

For people who own property in the surrounding streets, including Tiong Bahru, Tanjong Pagar and the broader Outram area, this adds another future luxury project to an already-growing pipeline of high-end supply nearby. More supply in the same price segment over time could moderate how much individual units in the area can appreciate. That is not an immediate concern, but it is worth keeping in mind as you think about the long-term trajectory of values in that part of the city.

Outram Road上的Tan Boon Liat Building，长期以来以家具和室内设计商家的聚集地著称，如今已通过en bloc集体出售，以$950M易手。这块地段之所以与众不同，在于它属于永久地契，在新加坡市区中心，如此体量的永久地契土地实属罕见。

在Outram Park MRT附近拥有永久地契土地，毗邻Greater Southern Waterfront走廊和丹戎巴葛一带，正是发展商愿意付出溢价争夺的资产类型。外界普遍预期这块地段将被重新发展为高端住宅项目，或许还会附带部分零售或商业空间。仅土地部分就耗资$950M，未来落成的住宅售价势必相当可观。

对于拥有周边街道物业的人士，包括Tiong Bahru、丹戎巴葛以及更广泛的Outram一带，这笔交易在该区域已不断增长的高端供应管道中又添了一个未来豪华项目。同一价格区间的供应持续增加，长远来看可能会压制个别单位在该区域的升值空间。这并非眼前的迫切问题，但在思考这片城区的长期价值走势时，值得放在心里。
