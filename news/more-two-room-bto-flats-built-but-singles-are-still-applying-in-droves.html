More Two-Room BTO Flats Built, But Singles Are Still Applying in Droves两房式BTO单位供应增加，单身人士申请热情依然不减

The government has been steadily increasing the number of two-room flexi BTO flats set aside exclusively for singles, responding to years of feedback that this group had too little access to the public housing system. On paper, more supply sounds like good news. The problem is that demand has been growing just as fast, meaning the application rate per flat has barely budged.

Singles are a large and expanding segment of buyers, and for most of them the two-room flexi flat is the only direct route into BTO. So even as HDB adds more of these units to each sales exercise, the queue stays long and the ballot odds remain rough.

For singles in the market right now, the honest advice is this: do not assume that more supply automatically translates into a better chance for you personally. You may still need to apply multiple times before getting a queue number, and the wait after that can stretch several years.

If you have already been rejected a few times, it is worth looking seriously at the resale market as a backup. Grants are available for eligible singles buying resale, and the wait is immediate. BTO may still be the right long-term call, but go in with clear eyes about how long the road actually is.

政府一直在持续增加专门为单身人士保留的两房式BTO单位数量，回应多年来外界反映这群人进入公共住房体系的渠道太少的声音。从数字上看，供应增加听起来是好消息。问题在于需求同样在快速增长，意味着每个单位的申请率几乎没有下降。

单身人士是买家群体中一个庞大且持续扩大的群体，对大多数人来说，两房式BTO单位是直接申请BTO的唯一途径。因此，即使HDB在每次销售活动中增加这类单位的数量，排队依然漫长，抽签机率依然偏低。

对于目前在市场上找房的单身人士，坦诚的建议是：不要以为供应增加就自动意味着你个人的中签机会变大。你可能仍然需要多次申请才能排到号，之后的等待也可能长达数年。

如果你已经被拒绝几次，认真考虑转向二手市场作为备选方案是值得的。符合资格的单身人士购买二手组屋可获得津贴，而且等待时间短得多。BTO从长远来看也许仍是正确的选择，但要对这条路究竟有多长保持清醒。
