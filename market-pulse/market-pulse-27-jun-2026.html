Resale flats are out-earning condos for landlords: 6.1% vs 3.6%.

27 June 2026 · 1-min read · by PropSight

Condo prices are holding steady, led by the suburbs and the areas just outside the city. HDB resale is holding steady. The priciest central areas have cooled a little.

Here is the number that stopped me mid-conversation with a client this week. A resale flat is earning around 6.1% a year in rent, at roughly $3,200 a month. A condo is pulling about 3.6%, at around $4,250 a month. The condo collects more rent in dollar terms, but you paid far more for it. Divide the rent by what you actually spent, and the flat wins clearly. For landlords right now, the heartland is quietly the stronger earner.

The flat works harder for every dollar you put in.

If you are buying to rent out, this gap matters more than it looks. Many buyers gravitate toward condos because the monthly rent number is higher and the project feels more aspirational. But yield is what services your mortgage and puts money in your pocket. A 6.1% return versus 3.6% is not a rounding difference. If steady rental income is your main goal, the case for a heartland resale flat is real this week, not just on paper.

For sellers, where you are makes a big difference. The most expensive central areas are cooling. But places like Toa Payoh are up 5.6% and Bukit Merah is up 4.2%, so if you are in those parts of town, conditions are still working in your favour. One quiet piece of news worth knowing if you own an older development: the owners of Loyang Valley recently sold their whole estate to a developer in one collective deal, for around $880 million. When a deal that size lands, it tends to reset what buyers and developers will pay for nearby land, and can quietly lift what your own block could fetch even if nothing about your unit has changed. If your development is getting on in years and this kind of news is landing close to home, it pays to get a proper read on your value before conditions shift. The simple takeaway this week: own in the heartland, and the market is doing more for you than the headlines suggest.
