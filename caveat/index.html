How to use Caveat

Six tools, one source of truth, official Singapore transaction data. Here's the quick tour.

  1. 1
    Valuation, price a home in seconds.

    Choose HDB or Condo for a full valuation deck, estimate range, comparable sales, price trend, map, amenities, rental yield (condos) and a written summary. Landed shows recent reference transactions and New launch shows the new-sale benchmark (these are priced differently, so they're not a single estimate).

  2. 2
    Market, read the whole market.

    A live dashboard of median prices, price-per-sqft momentum, the rental market and gross yields, plus a sortable breakdown by town and the most-active condos.

  3. 3
    Find, search by budget & yield.

    Tell it a region, max budget and minimum rental yield; it ranks the condos (or towns) that fit. Tap Value this to jump straight into a full valuation.

  4. 4
    Affordability, qualify a buyer.

    Enter the buyer's profile, price and income. Caveat works out ABSD, stamp duty, cash upfront, the maximum loan and whether it passes the TDSR/MSR limits, and shows exactly which rules it applied.

  5. 5
    Upgrade, plan an HDB → condo move.

    Enter your flat and a target condo; it maps your sale proceeds, both stamp-duty paths (sell-first vs buy-first), the loan you'd qualify for and the real cash you'd need.

  6. 6
    Prospecting, find leads in your farm area.

    See what just sold in a town/district (with a drafted outreach message to copy), or list MOP targets, blocks reaching their 5-year mark, full of first-time sellers.

What Caveat covers, and what it doesn't

  • ✓ Full valuations for HDB resale flats & private condo/apartment resale
  • ✓ Landed shown as recent reference transactions & new launches as a new-sale benchmark (not a single estimate, they're priced differently)
  • ✕ Estimates are indicative from recent comparables, not a bank or official valuation
  • ✓ Data is official (URA, HDB, OneMap), auto-refreshed weekly
