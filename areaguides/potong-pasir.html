Potong Pasir

What it's like

Potong Pasir is the rare central estate that stayed low-rise and low-key, with its own MRT and a character quite unlike the Toa Payoh core beside it.

Potong Pasir sits in District 13 along the Kallang River, officially a subzone of the Toa Payoh planning area but understood by most Singaporeans as its own neighbourhood. The signature is the low-rise HDB estate on Potong Pasir Avenue, blocks with the distinctive sloping roofs that make it one of the few genuinely low-density public housing pockets left on the island. Next to it runs the Sennett Estate, a landed enclave of terrace and semi-detached homes. The result is a corner of the central region that feels established and quiet, not the dense high-rise pattern you get one stop away.

The estate has its own North-East Line station, Potong Pasir (NE10), which puts town within a short ride: Dhoby Ghaut and the Orchard interchange are a handful of stops down the line. The St Andrew's Village cluster is right here, with St Andrew's Junior School, Secondary School and Junior College, which anchors family demand. It is also worth stating plainly that Potong Pasir was Chiam See Tong's opposition ward for 27 years, a fact that is part of how residents and long-time Singaporeans think about the place. Housing runs across the range: HDB flats at a four-room resale median around $750k, older private blocks, and a wave of newer condos like The Tre Ver and The Poiz Residences that came up around Woodleigh and the edge of the new Bidadari estate one stop away.

What you trade for the quiet is convenience of scale. Potong Pasir does not have a large mall or a big hawker centre of its own, so day-to-day shopping and eating leans on Toa Payoh next door and the growing Bidadari and Woodleigh amenities one stop away. Parts of the HDB estate are ageing, and the low-rise design that gives it character also means fewer of the newer facilities packed into taller, denser towns. But for buyers who want an established central address with its own MRT, the St Andrew's schools, the Kallang River park connector on the doorstep and a calmer feel than the Toa Payoh core, the trade reads clearly in Potong Pasir's favour.

The honest view

What works

  • Its own North-East Line station, so town is a short ride while the estate stays quiet and low-rise.
  • The St Andrew's Village schools are right here, and the Sennett landed enclave gives the area a settled, established feel.
  • A calmer, more characterful pocket than the dense Toa Payoh core, with the Kallang River park connector on the doorstep.

Worth weighing

  • Parts of the HDB estate are ageing, with the shorter remaining leases that come with an older town.
  • Limited retail of its own, so shopping and eating lean on Toa Payoh next door and Bidadari one stop away.
  • The low-rise design means fewer of the newer facilities you get in taller, denser estates.
