Siglap

What it's like

Siglap is where east-side families buy a landed home or a large older seafront condo near the beach, and the market runs on landed money.

Siglap sits on the east coast in District 15, officially part of the Bedok planning area but a distinct place with its own name and its own feel. The core is landed: leafy streets on and around Siglap Hill and out towards Frankel, lined with terraces, semi-detached houses and bungalows that trade for serious money. Around them stand the big, long-standing condominiums that the area is known for, projects like Mandarin Gardens, Neptune Court, Laguna Park and Bayshore, most of them built decades ago and sitting close to the sea. There is effectively no HDB here, so this is a private market from the ground up, and the pricing follows.

The numbers tell you what kind of address this is. Landed homes carry a median of about $6.70m across 108 recent sales, which is the real headline in Siglap and the reason the neighbourhood reads as a landed enclave first. The condos give a more reachable way in, with a resale median around $1.84m, or roughly $1,476 psf, and a gross rental yield near 3.1%. Day to day, life runs off Siglap Centre and the Siglap Road strip for food, with East Coast Road and its shops and restaurants a short drive on, and East Coast Park across the ECP for the beach, the cycling paths and the sea. The schools carry weight too, with Victoria School, St Patrick's, Tao Nan and CHIJ Katong all nearby, which is a large part of why east-side families plant themselves here.

For most of its history Siglap was a car neighbourhood, and getting to town meant the ECP or a bus. The Thomson-East Coast Line changed that, with Siglap and Marine Terrace stations now putting the area a direct ride from the city, a real shift from the old days. The honest trade-offs are clear. Landed and freehold condos here are expensive, and there is no cheaper flat to fall back on. Many of the condos are older and physically large, so the quantum is high even when the psf looks fair, and several face their own upgrading cycles. Some stacks catch traffic noise off the ECP, and the MRT only just arrived, so the neighbourhood is still adjusting to being on the rail map. You buy Siglap for the landed streets, the sea and the space, and you pay for all three.

The honest view

What works

  • A leafy, established landed enclave on the east coast, with terraces and bungalows around Siglap Hill and Frankel, plus large seafront condos near the beach.
  • East Coast Park and the sea a short hop across the ECP, the Siglap Road and East Coast Road food nearby, and well-regarded schools like Victoria School, St Patrick's and Tao Nan.
  • Now on the Thomson-East Coast Line at Siglap and Marine Terrace, after decades as a car-only neighbourhood.

Worth weighing

  • Landed and freehold condos are expensive, with a landed median around $6.70m and no cheaper HDB option to fall back on.
  • Many of the condos are older and physically large, so the quantum is high even where the psf looks reasonable, and several face their own upgrading cycles.
  • Some stacks catch traffic noise off the ECP, and the MRT only just arrived, so the area is still adjusting to being on the rail map.
