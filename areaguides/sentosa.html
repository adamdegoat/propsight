Sentosa

Singapore's only resort-residential enclave, a gated marina community of waterfront condos and bungalows in District 4, and the one address where a foreigner can buy landed property.

What it's like

Sentosa is a lifestyle and second-home address more than a normal neighbourhood, bought for the marina, the berths and the beaches, not for the numbers.

Sentosa sits off the mainland as the Southern Islands planning area, and for buyers it means two things: Sentosa Cove and the resort island around it. The Cove is a gated marina community of waterfront condominiums and bungalows, many with a private berth at the water's edge, wrapped around a man-made marina in District 4. There is no HDB here at all, so this is a private market from the ground up, and an unusual one. Sentosa Cove is the only place in Singapore where a foreigner can buy landed property, subject to government approval, which gives it a buyer pool that no other district has.

What people buy into is the setting and the lifestyle. The homes sit next to the beaches, two golf courses, Resorts World and the casino, and the marina berths let owners keep a boat at the doorstep. It reads as a second-home or waterfront-lifestyle address for people who want to be away from the city rather than in it. Access is its own world: you reach the island over the Sentosa Boardwalk, on the Sentosa Express monorail, or across the Sentosa Gateway by car, and the nearest MRT is HarbourFront on the mainland. Green and leisure space is not the problem here, with the beaches, Fort Siloso and the golf all close by.

The honest catch is that this is a hard market to own. Sentosa Cove prices corrected sharply after their 2012 peak and still sit below it, and the market is illiquid, so both buying well and selling on can take time. The location that feels like a retreat is also cut off from the mainland, the central business district and the top-school catchments, which rules it out for most families. Maintenance and running costs are high, rental yields are thin at around 2.2 percent, and day to day the place can feel quiet and touristy rather than a lived-in neighbourhood. You buy here for the water, the berth and the lifestyle, not for the sums on a spreadsheet.

Who it suitsBuyers after a waterfront lifestyle or a second home, an owner who wants a private berth for a boat, or a foreigner after the rare chance to own landed here, rather than anyone chasing yield or a normal family neighbourhood.

The honest view

What works

  • The only resort-residential enclave in Singapore, with waterfront homes, private marina berths, and the beaches and golf a few minutes away.
  • Sentosa Cove is the one place a foreigner can buy landed property here, which makes it rare ground with a buyer pool no other district has.
  • A gated, low-density setting next to Resorts World, the beaches and two golf courses, if a lifestyle address is what you are after.

Worth weighing

  • An illiquid market that corrected hard and still sits below its 2012 peak, so buying well and selling on can both take time.
  • Cut off from the mainland, the central business district and the top-school catchments, with the nearest MRT at HarbourFront across the water.
  • High maintenance and running costs, and thin rental yields around 2.2 percent, so the sums rarely work as an investment.
