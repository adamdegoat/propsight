Katong

What it's like

Katong is the East Coast name people actually search for, bought for heritage, food and the sea rather than a bargain.

Katong sits in District 15 on the east side, officially part of the Marine Parade planning area but known and searched for by its own name far more than its parent's. It is Peranakan heartland: rows of conserved shophouses, the painted terraces on Koon Seng Road, and a food strip along East Coast Road and Joo Chiat that runs from decades-old laksa stalls to newer cafes and restaurants. The housing is mostly private, freehold landed homes and condominiums with a layer of restored shophouses, and only a small pocket of HDB nearby in Marine Parade and Marine Terrace. This is a private market first, and the pricing reflects it.

What people pay for is the address and the life around it. i12 Katong anchors the shopping, East Coast Park sits across the ECP for the beach and the cycling paths, and the schools carry weight: Tao Nan, CHIJ Katong Primary and Tanjong Katong draw families who plan years ahead. The seafront belt along Amber and Meyer holds some of the most expensive private stock in the east, projects like Amber Park, One Amber, The Esta and Silversea, where a resale condo runs around $2.27m, or roughly $2,090 psf. Alongside them, the older Marine Parade and Marine Terrace flats give a smaller pool of buyers a far cheaper way in, with four-room resale around $668k. Two very different budgets, one neighbourhood.

For most of its history Katong had no MRT, and residents lived on buses and the ECP. The Thomson-East Coast Line changed that, with Marine Parade and Marine Terrace stations now putting the area a direct ride from the city, a real shift that is already priced in. The honest trade-offs are straightforward. Freehold here is expensive, several of the seafront condos are older and due for their own upgrading cycles, some stacks catch traffic noise off the ECP, and the food strip pulls tourist and weekend crowds that locals learn to time around. You buy Katong for the heritage, the food and the sea, and you pay for all three.

Who it suitsFamilies, foodies and expats who want heritage, food and the East Coast lifestyle, buying into condos or landed homes, plus a smaller group after the more affordable Marine Parade HDB flats nearby.

The honest view

What works

  • One of Singapore's strongest place-brands: Peranakan heritage, the Koon Seng Road terraces and a food strip that runs from old laksa stalls to modern restaurants.
  • East Coast Park and the beach a walk away across the ECP, i12 Katong for shopping, and well-regarded schools like Tao Nan and Tanjong Katong.
  • Now on the Thomson-East Coast Line at Marine Parade and Marine Terrace, after decades with no MRT at all.

Worth weighing

  • Freehold and condo prices are high, with a condo median around $2.27m in District 15.
  • Several of the seafront condos along Amber and Meyer are older and face their own upgrading cycles, and some stacks catch ECP traffic noise.
  • The MRT only arrived recently, and the East Coast Road food strip draws tourist and weekend crowds.
