Queenstown

Singapore's first satellite town, now a gentrified central estate where the Dawson flats set national HDB price records and everything sits minutes from the CBD and one-north.

What it's like

Queenstown is the oldest satellite town in Singapore, and today one of the most expensive places to buy an HDB flat anywhere on the island.

Queenstown was Singapore's first satellite town, laid out and built through the 1950s and 60s before anyone had heard of Toa Payoh or Ang Mo Kio. Districts like Tanglin Halt, Commonwealth and Mei Ling grew up as modest workers' housing on the edge of the city. That history is still visible in the low older blocks that survive here, but the estate around them has changed completely. Sitting just west of the central districts, Queenstown has gentrified into one of the most sought-after central addresses that still carries an HDB postcode.

The housing is a study in contrasts. Newer Dawson projects, SkyVille @ Dawson, SkyTerrace @ Dawson and Forfar Heights, are among the most striking public housing in the country, and their resale prices have pushed the whole town to record highs. A four-room here runs about $1.01m at the median, so million-dollar flats are routine rather than remarkable, and yields sit near 4.9%. Buyers are a mix of families who want a central base for schools and work, and owner-occupiers who traded a private budget for a bigger, better-located flat. Private stock is prime too, with condo medians around $1.98m at roughly $2,170 psf and yields near 2.6%.

Location is the whole argument. Queenstown, Commonwealth and Redhill sit on the East-West line, the Circle line runs through the fringe, and you are a few stops from Raffles Place, one-north, Biopolis and Science Park. Anchorpoint, Queensway Shopping Centre and IKEA Alexandra are close, and the Southern Ridges and Alexandra Canal give you real green space for a central estate. The honest catch is the price of all this: you pay a heavy central premium, brand-new blocks stand next to leases that are decades older, and rental yields on both HDB and private are thin for what you put in.

Who it suitsBuyers who want a genuinely central address with strong transport and are willing to pay top dollar, whether for a record-price Dawson flat or prime private stock, and who care more about location than yield.

The honest view

What works

  • Genuinely central, with a few MRT stops to the CBD and minutes to one-north, Biopolis and Science Park.
  • Strong, established estate with the record-setting Dawson flats, good schools and real green space in the Southern Ridges and Alexandra Canal.
  • HDB values have held at record highs, so owners here have seen their flats appreciate hard.

Worth weighing

  • You pay a heavy central premium: a four-room runs about $1.01m, among the priciest HDB anywhere in Singapore.
  • Rental yields are thin, near 4.9% on HDB and only about 2.6% on private, so this is a place to live rather than a cash-flow play.
  • The stock is uneven, with brand-new blocks standing next to much older ones, so lease and condition vary a lot street to street.
