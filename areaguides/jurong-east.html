Jurong East

The commercial anchor of the west and Singapore's planned second CBD, with a dense mall cluster at one of the busiest MRT interchanges and Jurong Lake Gardens next door.

What it's like

Cheap flats, strong rents and a growth story: Jurong East runs on the jobs hub being built around it.

Jurong East is the commercial anchor of the west and the site of Singapore's designated second Central Business District, the Jurong Lake District. Around the MRT interchange sits one of the densest retail clusters outside town: JEM, Westgate, IMM and JCube, which is being redeveloped. Wrapped around them are Jurong Lake Gardens, the old Chinese and Japanese Gardens with their pagodas and stone bridges, and the Science Centre. For a suburb this far out, it has an unusual amount pulling people in, and a lot more planned as the district builds up.

The housing story is what makes it stand out for buyers. HDB here is cheap by Singapore standards, with a four-room median around $547k, while rental demand from the surrounding jobs hub keeps rents strong, roughly $3,500 a month on a four-room. That combination pushes the gross HDB yield to about 7.7%, among the highest in the country. It draws two kinds of buyer: owner-occupiers who want an affordable, well-connected flat, and investors chasing rental income they cannot get in pricier towns. Private stock clusters around Jurong Lake, from J Gateway right at the station to the older Lakeside condos.

Transport is the reason the numbers work. Jurong East interchange is where the North-South and East-West lines meet, one of the busiest stations on the network, and the Jurong Region Line is being added, with a possible cross-border rail link to Malaysia also on the cards. The honest catch is distance: this is the far west, and the ride into the traditional Raffles Place CBD is a long one. That trade-off is real today, though it is exactly what the Jurong Lake District plan is meant to close as more offices move out here.

Who it suitsBuyers who want an affordable, well-connected flat with strong rental demand, and investors betting on the Jurong Lake District growth story, if they can accept the far-west location.

The honest view

What works

  • One of the highest HDB rental yields in Singapore, around 7.7% on a four-room, on the back of strong demand from the jobs hub.
  • Affordable flats for a town this well connected, with a four-room median near $547k.
  • A major interchange where two MRT lines meet, the Jurong Region Line being added, and the Jurong Lake District growing around it.

Worth weighing

  • It sits out west, a long way from the traditional CBD, though the second-CBD plan is meant to change that.
  • Condos are a big step up in price for yields near 3.5%, well below what the HDB flats return.
  • A lot of the upside is a future story: the Jurong Lake District is still being built out, not finished.
