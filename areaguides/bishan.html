What it's like

Bishan is where families pay up to sit in the catchment of some of Singapore's best-known schools.

Bishan sits almost dead centre on the island, a mature estate built through the late 1980s that has aged into one of the most established addresses on the map. What really drives it is schools. Raffles Institution and Catholic High are here, along with Ai Tong, Kuo Chuan Presbyterian and Whitley Secondary, and families move from across the island to sit inside those catchments. That single fact shapes everything: demand stays high, flats change hands quickly, and asking prices hold firm even when the wider resale market softens.

The housing is mostly HDB, and it is not cheap. Bishan is one of the towns where million-dollar flats have become routine rather than a headline, especially the larger and higher units near the MRT and the top primary schools. Private stock is limited but well regarded: a handful of condos like Sky Habitat, Sky Vue, Bishan 8 and Clover By The Park, most of them mid to upper priced. The people who buy here tend to be settled families and upgraders who want the location and the schools, not investors chasing yield. That is why a $795k four-room resale and a $2.5m condo can sit a few streets apart.

Getting around is the other half of the appeal. Bishan is a North-South and Circle line interchange, so you can be at Raffles Place or Orchard in around 15 minutes, and the Circle line loops you across to the east and west without going through town. Junction 8 covers day-to-day shopping right at the station, and Bishan-Ang Mo Kio Park, one of the largest parks in Singapore, runs along the estate's edge. The honest catch is the premium. You pay clearly above the norm for the schools and the location, the flats are older with the leases that come with a 1980s estate, and new supply is thin, so prices hold firm.

The honest view

What works

  • Inside the catchment of some of Singapore's best-known schools, which keeps demand and resale prices steady.
  • One of the most central HDB towns, about 15 minutes to the city on two MRT lines.
  • Bishan-Ang Mo Kio Park on the doorstep and Junction 8 right at the station for daily needs.

Worth weighing

  • You pay a clear premium for the location and the schools, million-dollar flats are common here.
  • Flats are older, with the shorter remaining leases you'd expect from a 1980s estate.
  • Private stock is limited and yields are modest, near 2.8% on a condo, so it's not an investor's town.
