Marine Parade

A sea-facing eastern town where the original reclaimed-land HDB blocks sit next to Katong shophouses, Peranakan food and East Coast Park, now finally on the MRT after decades of buses.

What it's like

One of the few HDB towns where the flats and the beach share a fence line.

Marine Parade is Singapore's original seafront housing estate. The first blocks went up in the 1970s on land reclaimed from the sea, laid out to face East Coast Park, and a handful of the older flats still look straight out over the water. Wrapped around the estate are Katong and Joo Chiat, two of the best-preserved conservation districts in the country: rows of Peranakan shophouses on Koon Seng Road, old kopitiams, laksa stalls that have run for decades, and a newer layer of cafes and restaurants. Parkway Parade is the town-centre mall and the everyday anchor. It reads less like a heartland estate and more like a settled coastal neighbourhood with an HDB core.

The housing splits sharply. The flats are older, mostly 3, 4 and 5-room resale, and the four-room median sits around $668k, with the sea-facing and higher-floor units carrying a clear premium. Alongside them runs some of the most expensive private stock in the east: the Amber Road and Meyer belt holds Amber Park, One Amber, Silversea and The Sea View, where prices run past $2m and often well beyond. That draws two different buyers. Families and older Singaporeans stay for the food, the beach and the schools, while private buyers pay up for the address, the sea views and the district 15 postcode.

For most of its life the estate was bus-dependent, sitting off the rail map while the rest of the island got MRT. The Thomson-East Coast Line changed that: Marine Parade and Marine Terrace stations opened on the line and put the town a direct ride from Orchard and the city. That access is real and it is priced in. The honest trade-offs stay the same as before, the flats are old and the leases show it, and the sea-view units and the Amber condos cost a clear premium you pay upfront.

Who it suitsBuyers who want the sea, the beach and Katong food on their doorstep, and will accept older flats or a private-market premium to get the address.

The honest view

What works

  • A rare HDB estate on the sea, with East Coast Park and the beach a short walk away.
  • Strong Katong and Joo Chiat character: Peranakan shophouses, old hawker food and a settled neighbourhood feel.
  • Now on the Thomson-East Coast Line, a direct ride to the city after decades of buses.

Worth weighing

  • The flats are old, and the sea-facing and higher-floor units cost a real premium.
  • The private stock around Amber and Meyer is expensive, often past $2m for modest yields near 3%.
  • Until the TEL opened the town was bus-dependent, and some pockets still sit a walk from a station.
