Frankel

A planned 1950s garden-city landed estate in District 15, on the Bedok side of the East Coast, where Opera Estate's streets are named after operas and the homes are terraces, semi-detached and detached houses with almost no HDB.

What it's like

Landed, leafy and settled, Frankel is bought for a house in a quiet 1950s garden estate with strong schools, if you can afford the price.

Frankel sits in the Bedok planning area in District 15, a short way inland from Siglap and the East Coast. It was laid out in the 1950s as a garden-city estate, and it still reads that way: quiet residential streets, mature trees and rows of landed homes rather than towers. The adjoining Opera Estate is the part everyone remembers, with streets named after operas, from Aida and Carmen to Figaro, Tosca and Rigoletto. Frankel Avenue runs through the middle of it all, and the surrounding terraces, semi-detached and detached houses make up the bulk of the stock. There is essentially no HDB here, so this is a private, landed market from the ground up.

The headline number is the landed price. Across 181 recent landed sales the median came in at about $6.00m, which tells you what it costs to buy a house in this estate. Around the edges you get a thin layer of condominiums, most of them older and mid-sized, names like Flamingo Valley, Astoria Park, The Trumps and Siglap V, with a resale condo median near $1.62m, around $1,629 per square foot, and gross yields around 3.0%. Who buys in is fairly consistent: families who want a landed home, multi-generational owners who have held here for decades, and upgraders moving up from flats and condos nearby. The schools are a big part of the pull, with Ngee Ann Primary, St Stephen's, the Temasek family of Primary, Secondary and Junior College, and Maha Bodhi all in or near the estate.

Getting around used to be the honest weak point. Frankel was built for cars, and for years the nearest MRT was a real distance away. The Thomson-East Coast Line has changed that, with Marine Terrace and Siglap now within reach at the southern edge of the area, and the East Coast Parkway close by for drivers. Be clear-eyed on the trade-offs before you commit. Landed prices are high and landed homes are illiquid, so this is a large, long-term hold rather than something you trade in and out of. The condo options are limited and mostly older, so buyers who want a big modern condo will not find much here. And the estate itself is quiet at night, with the shops, malls and offices sitting outside its streets, which is exactly what many buyers here are paying for.

The honest view

What works

  • A planned 1950s garden estate of terraces, semi-detached and detached houses, quiet, leafy and settled, with Opera Estate's opera-named streets giving it real character.
  • Strong schools in and around the estate, including Ngee Ann Primary, St Stephen's, the Temasek Primary, Secondary and Junior College, and Maha Bodhi.
  • Now on the rail map, with the Thomson-East Coast Line nearby at Marine Terrace and Siglap, and East Coast Park and the East Coast Road food strip close by.
  • Landed supply here is scarce and tightly held, which has kept prices firm over the years.

Worth weighing

  • Landed prices are high, with a median around $6.00m, and landed homes are illiquid, so this is a large, long-term commitment.
  • Historically car-dependent, with no MRT at the door until the Thomson-East Coast Line opened nearby, so parts of the estate are still a walk from a train.
  • Condo choices are limited and mostly older, so buyers who want a large, modern condo have little to pick from here.
  • Quiet at night, with the everyday shops, malls and offices sitting outside the estate's streets.
