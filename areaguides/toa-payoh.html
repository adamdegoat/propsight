Toa Payoh

One of Singapore's oldest and most central heartland towns, about ten minutes to the city on the North-South Line, with the Dragon Playground and the HDB Hub at its core.

What it's like

Toa Payoh is a mature central town where a heartland feel meets city-fringe prices, and million-dollar flats are now routine.

Toa Payoh was Singapore's second HDB town, laid out in the 1960s, and it still carries that early-estate character: low-numbered lorongs, the round market at Lorong 8, the old Dragon Playground behind Block 28, and the HDB Hub sitting right on the town centre. Everything you need is within a short walk of the interchange, the library, a big food centre, wet markets, and rows of shophouses and coffee shops that have been trading for decades. It reads as a settled, unflashy heartland rather than a new estate, and locals treat it as their own patch.

The housing is mostly HDB, and it is expensive HDB. A four-room resale now runs around $990k, close to a million, and larger and better-placed units regularly cross that line. That price sits on top of a genuinely central location and a long list of established primary and secondary schools, which is why families and upgraders keep bidding. There is a smaller pocket of private condos along the lorongs, Gem Residences, Trevista, Oleander Towers and Trellis Towers among them, that draws buyers who want the same address with a facility deck.

Transport is the real draw. Toa Payoh and Braddell put you on the North-South Line, roughly ten minutes to Orchard and the city, with Caldecott, now a Circle Line and Thomson-East Coast Line interchange, a short ride away. The honest trade-off is what you pay for that: a clear central premium baked into every asking price, and a lot of stock in blocks that are 40-plus years old with shorter remaining leases, which matters for financing and for anyone planning a long hold.

Who it suitsBuyers who want a central address and short commute, value established schools and a settled heartland, and can stomach paying up for it.

The honest view

What works

  • Genuinely central, about ten minutes to the city on the North-South Line, with Caldecott, a Circle Line and Thomson-East Coast Line interchange, close by.
  • A mature, self-contained town with the HDB Hub, big food centres, wet markets and established schools.
  • Prices are holding at record levels, so resale HDB here has been a firm store of value.

Worth weighing

  • You pay a clear central premium, with four-room resale near $990k and million-dollar flats now common.
  • A lot of the HDB stock is 40-plus years old with shorter leases left, which affects financing and a long hold.
  • Condo yields are thin at around 3.0%, so the private stock is more a lifestyle buy than a rental play.
