What it's like

An old-money prime address bought to live in, where the value sits in the land and the trees rather than the rental yield.

Tanglin sits between Orchard and the Botanic Gardens, and it has been one of Singapore's most established prime addresses for a long time. This is embassy row and Good Class Bungalow country, quiet streets of large landed homes behind high walls, foreign missions along Nassim and Napier Roads, and a run of older and newer condominiums filling the gaps. There is no HDB here. It is a fully private district from the ground up, and the pricing reflects that.

The housing stock is landed-led, with rows of Good Class Bungalows and detached houses setting the tone, wrapped around condos like D'Leedon, Leedon Green, Spring Grove and Fourth Avenue Residences. That mix is why the condo median lands around $3.10m, roughly $2,269 psf in District 10, while the landed market pushes well beyond it. The people who buy here are mostly owner-occupiers, long-hold families and established money who want a green, central address, not investors working a spreadsheet. Rents are high in dollar terms, from about $6,000 a month, but against these prices the gross yield is thin, around 2.6%.

Getting into town has always been easy from here, and the Thomson-East Coast Line made it easier, with doors at Napier and Orchard Boulevard on the edges, and Botanic Gardens on the Downtown and Circle lines at the northern end. Dempsey Hill, the former army barracks turned restaurant, gallery and lifestyle enclave, gives the area its evenings-out draw. The honest catch is the price of admission and the pace. This is expensive, landed-driven ground, few MRT doors actually sit inside the area, and the residential streets go quiet at night, so it leans on driving and can feel sleepy. You buy here for the address, the space and the greenery, not for the numbers.

Who it suitsOwner-occupiers and families who want a quiet, green prime address next to town and can absorb District 10 prices, rather than investors chasing yield.

The honest view

What works

  • One of Singapore's most established prime addresses, embassy row and Good Class Bungalows that hold value through the cycles.
  • The Singapore Botanic Gardens, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, sits right beside it, with Dempsey Hill's restaurants and galleries close by.
  • Central and close to Orchard, now with Thomson-East Coast Line doors at Napier and Orchard Boulevard on the edges.

Worth weighing

  • Very expensive, the condo median sits around $3.10m and the landed market pushes well past that.
  • Yields are thin, roughly 2.6% gross, because this is owner-occupier ground rather than a rental play.
  • Few MRT doors sit inside the area itself and the streets go quiet at night, so it leans on driving and can feel sleepy.
