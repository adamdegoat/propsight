Central Area

Singapore's city core, from Marina Bay and Shenton Way down to Chinatown and Bugis, with the CBD, the waterfront and a knot of MRT lines all on the doorstep.

What it's like

This is as central as it gets in Singapore, and you pay for every metre of it.

The Central Area is the city itself, not a heartland town. It runs from the CBD towers along Shenton Way and Raffles Place, out to the Marina Bay waterfront and Gardens by the Bay, and back through Chinatown, Tanjong Pagar, Bugis and Rochor and the civic district around City Hall. People here live in two very different kinds of home: the glass condos of Marina Bay and Shenton Way, and a small number of centrally located HDB flats, such as the older blocks around Kreta Ayer and Chinatown, which almost never come up for sale. It is dense, walkable and fully urban, the opposite of a quiet suburb.

The HDB supply is tiny and rarely comes up, which is part of why it is the most expensive place in Singapore to buy a flat. A four-room here has a median around $1.18m, over a million dollars, more than double what the same flat fetches in most towns, driven by the scarcity and the sheer central location. Flats that do change hands still rent strongly, around $4,500 a month for a four-room, so the gross yield lands near 4.6%. Condos cover an enormous range. Older city-fringe units start around $1.5m, while Marina Bay and Shenton addresses run well above $3m, and the median across the area sits near $1.67m, about $1,992 psf. Buyers here are a mix of professionals who want to live above their office, investors chasing central rental demand, and a lucky few who secured one of the area's scarce central flats.

Transport is the easy part. The North-South, East-West, Downtown, North East, Circle and Thomson-East Coast lines all thread through the core, and interchanges like Raffles Place, Dhoby Ghaut, Chinatown and Marina Bay put most of the island within a single ride. You can walk to work in the CBD, walk to the waterfront in the evening, and have Chinatown and Bugis hawker food a few minutes away. The honest trade-off is the price and the setting. This is the costliest ground in Singapore, the flat supply is thin and unpredictable, and it is a dense, built-up city core rather than a family estate with fields and void-deck life.

Who it suitsBuyers who want to live inside the city, next to the CBD and the waterfront, and will pay a premium for the location over space, greenery and a family-town feel.

The honest view

What works

  • You live inside the city, walking distance to the CBD, the Marina Bay waterfront and Chinatown food.
  • Unmatched transport, with six MRT lines and major interchanges all in the core.
  • Scarce, centrally located flats hold their value and rent out easily.

Worth weighing

  • The most expensive place in Singapore to buy, with a four-room HDB median over $1.18m.
  • HDB supply is tiny and unpredictable, so flats rarely come onto the market.
  • Dense and fully urban, short on greenery, space and the family-estate feel of the heartlands, and condo yields near 2.8% are thin.
