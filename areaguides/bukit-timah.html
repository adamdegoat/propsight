Bukit Timah

Singapore's prime residential belt of Good Class Bungalows and condos, the country's densest cluster of top schools, and the Botanic Gardens and nature reserve on the doorstep.

What it's like

The address people pay up for, mostly for the schools and the greenery, with very little HDB in the mix.

Bukit Timah runs along Bukit Timah Road and Dunearn Road, and it has long been the most established private-housing belt in Singapore. The stock is overwhelmingly landed, streets of Good Class Bungalows and semi-detached houses, wrapped around older and newer condominiums along Sixth Avenue, Dunearn and Toh Tuck. Actual HDB is scarce here, limited mainly to the small pocket at Toh Yi Drive. So this is a private area first, and the pricing reflects that from the ground up.

The real magnet is the schools. The Bukit Timah cluster holds Nanyang Girls, Hwa Chong, National Junior College, Raffles Girls Primary, Methodist Girls and Singapore Chinese Girls, which is the densest run of brand-name schools in the country. That pulls in families who will pay a premium to live inside the one-kilometre and two-kilometre priority zones, and it holds resale values up even for the older, plainer condos. The buyers here are mostly owner-occupiers with school-age children and long holding horizons, not yield hunters. Greenery is the other draw: the Singapore Botanic Gardens, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, sits at the southern end, with Bukit Timah Nature Reserve and the Rail Corridor to the north.

Getting into town used to mean driving, but the Downtown Line changed that. Sixth Avenue, Tan Kah Kee, Botanic Gardens and King Albert Park now put much of the area on the MRT, and the Pan Island Expressway runs alongside for drivers. The honest catch is the price of admission. This is expensive, private-heavy ground, the landed and Good Class Bungalow market sets the tone, and even the rare HDB flat and the tired 1990s condos carry a prime-area premium that keeps rental yields low. You buy here for the schools, the address and the trees, not for the numbers on a spreadsheet.

Who it suitsFamilies chasing the top-school catchments and owner-occupiers who want a prime, leafy address and can absorb the price, rather than investors after yield.

The honest view

What works

  • The densest cluster of top schools in Singapore, which is why families pay up and values hold.
  • Prime, leafy and quiet, with the Botanic Gardens, Bukit Timah Nature Reserve and the Rail Corridor close by.
  • The Downtown Line now covers the area at four stations, so it is no longer car-only to reach town.

Worth weighing

  • Expensive across the board, the condo median sits around $2.42m and the landed market pushes prices higher.
  • Yields are thin, roughly 2.6% on a condo, because this is bought to live in, not to rent out.
  • Very little HDB stock, and the rare flat and older condos still carry a prime-area premium.
