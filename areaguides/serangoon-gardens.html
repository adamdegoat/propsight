What it's like

A settled landed estate bought for the house and the address, where the value sits in the land, the schools and the food rather than in rental yield.

Serangoon Gardens is one of Singapore's best known landed estates, laid out around the roundabout locals call Serangoon Garden Circus in District 19. The look is consistent: rows of red-roofed terrace houses and semi-detached homes on quiet streets with English names, low-rise and green, a world away from the towers around Serangoon MRT a short drive south. This is a landed market from the ground up. There is a little condo stock and essentially no HDB, so most buyers here are choosing a house, and the pricing follows. The landed median sits around $5.50m off 191 recent sales, which tells you the ground here is expensive.

What people buy into is the estate itself and what sits at its centre. Chomp Chomp Food Centre is a Singapore hawker institution, packed at night for satay, sotong and BBQ stingray, and myVillage mall covers the everyday shopping. The Serangoon Gardens Country Club, the shophouse rows around the Circus and a run of cafes give it a settled, established feel. Two things drive demand here: multi-generational families who have held these houses for decades, and expat families drawn by the international schools nearby, the Australian International School and Lycee Francais, with Rosyth on the local side. It reads as quiet, family and slightly old-money landed living rather than anywhere you go for nightlife.

The honest catch is access and liquidity. There is no MRT station inside the estate, so you drive or take a bus, with the nearest doors at Serangoon on the North-East and Circle lines and Lorong Chuan on the Circle Line, both a bus ride or drive away. Landed prices are high and the market is thin, so a house here is a slow, large commitment to buy or sell, not something you trade. If you want an apartment instead, the choice is narrow: only a couple of real condos, The Garden Residences and Kensington Park, where a unit runs around $1.40m at roughly $1,838 psf and grosses close to 2.9%. The streets are quiet at night, which is the point for some buyers and too sleepy for others. You buy here for the house, the estate and the food, not for the yield.

The honest view

What works

  • A genuine landed estate with real character, the red-roofed terrace and semi-detached houses around Serangoon Garden Circus, quiet and green.
  • Chomp Chomp Food Centre on the doorstep, one of Singapore's best known hawker centres, with myVillage mall and the country club close by.
  • Popular with expat families for the international schools nearby, the Australian International School and Lycee Francais, with Rosyth on the local side.

Worth weighing

  • No MRT station inside the estate, so you drive or take a bus to Serangoon or Lorong Chuan, which is a real daily consideration.
  • Landed prices are high, a median around $5.50m, and the market is thin, so a house here is a slow, large commitment to buy or sell.
  • Apartment options are very limited, only a couple of condos, and the condo yield near 2.9% is modest, so this is owner-occupier ground.
  • The streets go quiet at night, which suits some buyers and feels too sleepy for others.
