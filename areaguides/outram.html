Outram

The heritage edge of the CBD in District 2, where restored Tanjong Pagar and Keong Saik shophouses, Chinatown's temples and food, and Singapore's tallest homes all sit a short walk from the office towers.

What it's like

Central and almost entirely private, Outram is where people pay to live among heritage streets and walk to work in the financial district.

Outram covers Tanjong Pagar, Chinatown and Outram Park, on the western edge of the central business district in District 2. The housing here is mostly private and comes in two very different forms: rows of restored shophouses along Tanjong Pagar Road, Keong Saik and Duxton that have been turned into homes, offices and restaurants, and a run of newer condominiums such as Wallich Residence, Sky Everton and Spottiswoode Residences. There is a small HDB pocket around Kreta Ayer and the Chinatown streets, but it is a minor part of the picture, so this trades as a private, city-centre market from the ground up.

What people buy into is the walk to work and the setting. The office towers of the CBD start a few minutes away, Chinatown puts hawker food at Maxwell and Chinatown Complex and temples like the Buddha Tooth Relic and Sri Mariamman on the doorstep, and Guoco Tower crowns it with Wallich Residence, the tallest homes in the country. The buyers are mostly professionals and couples who want to live among heritage streets and skip the commute, rather than families chasing a big flat or a school catchment. Green space is thin but real: Pearl's Hill City Park sits on the rise behind Chinatown, Ann Siang Hill gives a pocket of calm, and the bay is a short hop south.

Getting around is the strong suit. Outram Park is one of the busiest interchanges in the system, where the East-West, North-East and Thomson-East Coast lines meet, and Tanjong Pagar and Chinatown stations sit at the edges. The honest catch is the price of admission and the setting. This is dense, built-up ground with almost no greenery, the older shophouses and walk-ups sit right next to shiny new launches, and units run compact at a high psf. Some streets are also still shaking off a seedier past. Note too that the well-known Pinnacle@Duxton flats sit just across the boundary in Bukit Merah, so they are not Outram HDB stock. You buy here for the address, the heritage and the walk to the office, not for space or greenery.

The honest view

What works

  • A genuinely central address in District 2, walkable to the CBD offices, with Outram Park putting three MRT lines at your door.
  • Heritage on the doorstep, restored Tanjong Pagar and Keong Saik shophouses, Chinatown's food and temples, and Singapore's tallest homes at Guoco Tower.
  • Yields sit a touch healthier than most prime areas, around 3.2% gross, helped by steady rental demand from professionals working in the CBD.

Worth weighing

  • Dense and built-up with almost no greenery, so you trade space and trees for the location.
  • Older shophouse and walk-up stock sits right next to new launches, and units run compact at a high psf.
  • Some streets are still shaking off a seedier past, and the mix of tired walk-ups and shiny towers is uneven block to block.
