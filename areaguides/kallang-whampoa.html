Kallang/Whampoa

A central, inner-city town wrapped around the Kallang River and the old Whampoa estate, about ten minutes from the city on three MRT lines.

What it's like

One of the few HDB towns you can live in and still walk to the edge of the city.

Kallang/Whampoa is a central town built along the Kallang River, running from Boon Keng and Bendemeer through the older Whampoa estate down to the Kallang sports belt. It carries two very different faces. Up in Whampoa it is a settled, low-key HDB neighbourhood of mature blocks, the well-worn Whampoa Makan Place food centre and a wet market, the river and its park connectors just behind. Down by the water it turns into the Singapore Sports Hub and the National Stadium, with newer riverside condos looking across the channel. For a town this close to town, it stays surprisingly quiet on the residential streets.

The HDB stock here is central and priced like it. A four-room resale runs around $909k, which is well above what the same flat fetches in the outer towns, and it buys you a ten-minute ride to the office rather than space. That draws two kinds of buyer: people who want to stay near the city core without paying condo money, and investors chasing the rental demand that a central address pulls in. The catch is the age of the estate. Many Whampoa and Bendemeer blocks date back decades, so leases are shorter than a fresh BTO, and that has to sit in your sums if you are borrowing or planning a long hold.

Transport is the real draw. Boon Keng sits on the North-East Line, Bendemeer on the Downtown Line, and Stadium and Mountbatten on the Circle Line, so most of the town is a short walk from a train and roughly ten minutes from the central business district. The honest trade-off is the flip side of being this central: it is a dense inner-city estate rather than a spacious suburb, the flats are not cheap for HDB, and the older blocks come with the lease question attached. You are paying for the location, and the location delivers.

Who it suitsBuyers who want a genuinely central address on a fast commute, and will trade suburban space and a fresh lease for it.

The honest view

What works

  • Genuinely central: roughly ten minutes to the city on three MRT lines from Boon Keng, Bendemeer, Stadium and Mountbatten.
  • Steady central rental demand keeps a four-room grossing around 5.0%, solid for a spot this close to town.
  • River, park connectors, the Sports Hub and the Whampoa food centre and market all close at hand.

Worth weighing

  • Pricey for HDB: a four-room resale near $909k is well above the outer towns for less space.
  • Many Whampoa and Bendemeer blocks are old, so leases are shorter, which matters for financing and a long hold.
  • A dense inner-city estate rather than a roomy suburb, and condos step up to modest yields near 3.1%.
