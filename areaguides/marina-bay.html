Marina Bay

District 1 living inside the Downtown Core, wrapped around Marina Bay, Raffles Place and Shenton Way, where the homes are private high-rise towers and the office district is the neighbourhood.

What it's like

The rare address where home sits inside the financial district and on the water, bought by professionals who want to walk to work and investors who rent to them.

Marina Bay is the residential face of the Downtown Core, Singapore's central business district and District 1. The homes are private towers rising straight out of the office grid, from The Sail and Marina Bay Residences on the waterfront to Marina One Residences and V on Shenton over towards Shenton Way. There is no HDB here at all, and very little that looks like a normal neighbourhood. This is a place built for work first and living second, and the housing stock reflects that: high-rise, compact and priced for the address.

What people buy into is the location. Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, the Esplanade and the towers of the financial district are all a short walk from the front door, and the water is the view. The buyers are professionals who want to step out of a lift and into the office, and investors who buy a one or two-bedroom unit to rent to the banking and finance crowd that fills these buildings during the week. That tenant demand is the quiet strength of the area: rental yields sit near 3 percent gross, which is healthier than most of the prime districts, where owners struggle to clear 2.5 percent.

Getting around is as easy as it gets, with Raffles Place, Marina Bay, Downtown and Shenton Way stations putting several MRT lines within a few minutes of each other. The honest catch is that this is a workplace that people also happen to live in. Prices per square foot are high, around $1,992 psf, the units are mostly small and investor-sized, and the ground floor is offices and tourists rather than wet markets and everyday shops. It is busy with visitors around Marina Bay Sands and the promenade, then it empties out and goes quiet at weekends when the office crowd leaves. You buy here to walk to work, for the water and for the rental demand, not for space or a settled neighbourhood feel.

The honest view

What works

  • Home sits inside the CBD and on the water, with Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, the Esplanade and the financial district all a short walk away.
  • Strong rental demand from the finance crowd keeps yields near 3 percent gross, healthier than most prime districts.
  • Several MRT lines meet here at Raffles Place, Marina Bay, Downtown and Shenton Way, so the rest of the island is within easy reach.

Worth weighing

  • Expensive per square foot, around $1,992 psf, and mostly compact investor units rather than family-sized homes.
  • Tourist-heavy around Marina Bay Sands and the promenade, and it goes quiet at weekends once the office crowd leaves.
  • Thin on heartland amenities, with few wet markets and everyday shops for day-to-day living.
