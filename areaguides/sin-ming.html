What it's like

Sin Ming and Upper Thomson is where people buy for the food and the greenery, across landed homes, Thomson condos and a small pocket of HDB.

Sin Ming and Upper Thomson sit in the top corner of the Bishan planning area, District 20, and the address is really three things stitched together. There is the Upper Thomson food belt, the Thomson Road eateries, Casuarina roti prata, the old Thomson coffee shops and Shunfu Mart, that people drive in from across the island for. There is the Sin Ming pocket itself, a mix of landed homes and a light-industrial strip of auto workshops and car showrooms that has been there for decades. And there is the green edge: Thomson Nature Park, MacRitchie Reservoir and the Central Catchment right on the doorstep. It is a food-led, nature-adjacent address rather than a school-chasing one like Bishan proper next door.

The housing is genuinely mixed, which is unusual. Along Thomson you have the condos, Thomson Three, Thomson Grand, Thomson Impressions and The Gardens at Bishan, with resale medians around $2.19m, roughly $1,822 psf. Behind them sit the Sin Ming landed terraces and a small, older HDB pocket where a four-room trades around $729k, near $679 psf. That range means a young couple after a compact condo, a family after a landed home and an HDB buyer priced out of Bishan centre can all be looking in the same postcode. The trade-off is on yield: condos here rent from about $4,500 a month but the gross yield sits near 2.5%, so this is a live-in address, not an investor's play. The HDB pocket does better at around 5.9%, but it is small and there is not much of it to buy.

The big change is transport. For years Sin Ming leaned hard on the car, and parts of it still do because the estate is spread out. The Thomson-East Coast Line has now put stations at Upper Thomson and Bright Hill, right next to the Thomson condos, which finally gives the area a proper train link into town and up towards Woodlands. Bishan and Ang Mo Kio are both next door for malls, hawker centres and the wider MRT network. The honest catch is that Sin Ming is not a tidy, self-contained town: the food, the green and the homes are scattered, some streets are workshops rather than shophouses, and away from the two MRT stations you still reach for the car.

The honest view

What works

  • The Upper Thomson food belt on the doorstep, from Casuarina prata and the old Thomson coffee shops to Shunfu Mart.
  • Green on every side, Thomson Nature Park, MacRitchie Reservoir and the Central Catchment, which is rare this close to the centre.
  • The Thomson-East Coast Line now serves it at Upper Thomson and Bright Hill, with Bishan and Ang Mo Kio next door.

Worth weighing

  • Condo yields are thin, near 2.5% gross, so this works as a home rather than an investment.
  • The HDB pocket is small and older, so there is not much stock and leases are shorter.
  • Parts of Sin Ming are light-industrial with auto workshops and showrooms, and the estate is spread out, so away from the two MRT stations you lean on the car.
