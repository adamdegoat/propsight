Choa Chu Kang

A large, affordable family town in the far north-west, built around Lot One and the Choa Chu Kang interchange, with the Kranji countryside and Sungei Buloh wetlands on its doorstep.

What it's like

You buy in Choa Chu Kang for the value and the space, and you pay for it with the commute.

Choa Chu Kang sits at the far north-west corner of the island, past Bukit Panjang and up against the Kranji countryside. It grew up in the 1990s as a value town and still reads that way: rows of HDB blocks, a busy town centre stacked on top of the MRT and LRT interchange, and Lot One Shoppers' Mall doing most of the shopping, eating and errands in one building. Yew Tee, one station up the North-South Line, works as a smaller second centre with its own market and coffee shops. It is a settled, unflashy place that families move to and stay in.

The housing is overwhelmingly HDB, and cheap by island standards. A four-room resale flat runs around $558k, which is roughly $100k under what the same flat costs in Tampines or Bishan, and the rental yield on it is high at about 6.7%. Private choice is thin: a handful of older condos like Yew Mei Green, Palm Gardens and Northvale, plus two newer executive condominiums, Sol Acres and Wandervale, near Keat Hong. The buyers are mostly young families and upgraders who want more flat for the money, people who work in Jurong, Woodlands or the Kranji industrial belt, and owners who like being next to the Choa Chu Kang Park, the park connectors and the greenery of Sungei Buloh.

Transport is the trade-off you sign up for. Choa Chu Kang interchange puts you on the North-South Line and the Bukit Panjang LRT loops through the estate, and the coming Jurong Region Line will add a second heavy-rail line here. But the ride into Raffles Place still runs around 45 minutes, and you feel the distance every day. Drivers use the BKE and KJE, which are quick to Woodlands and Jurong but a long haul to the city. One more honest note: the town backs onto one of the island's main cemeteries and columbaria at Choa Chu Kang, which some buyers do not mind and others rule out entirely.

The honest view

What works

  • Cheap for the space: a four-room flat around $558k, well under the mature-town price.
  • For landlords the numbers are hard to beat, a four-room grosses close to 6.7%, among the island's best.
  • Green and open, next to Choa Chu Kang Park, the park connectors and the Kranji countryside.

Worth weighing

  • A genuinely long commute, this is the far north-west and the city is about 45 minutes out.
  • Very little private choice, just a few older condos and two executive condominiums.
  • One of the island's main cemeteries and columbaria sits at the edge of the town, which some buyers rule out.
