Lavender

A city-edge strip around Lavender MRT and Jalan Besar, where mixed-use condos sit over the Kallang River a stop or two from the CBD, in District 12/8.

What it's like

Live one or two MRT stops from the office without paying Marina Bay or Tanjong Pagar prices.

Lavender is the city-edge strip that runs around Lavender MRT on the East-West Line and down Jalan Besar, part of the wider Kallang/Whampoa town in District 12/8. It reads as two things at once. Along the main roads you get mixed-use condos, City Gate, Southbank, Citylights and Kallang Riverside, towers with shops and food on the ground floor that look out over the Kallang River. Step back off the arterials and you are into the old Jalan Besar shophouses, a small pocket of HDB, Little India a short walk one way and Bugis the other. It is dense and urban rather than leafy, but it is genuinely close to town.

The pull here is location for the money. A resale condo runs around a $1.55m median, roughly $1,991 psf, which is real money but well under what the same unit costs in Marina or Tanjong Pagar for a commute that is only a few stops longer. Most of the stock is compact, one and two-bedroom units aimed at people who want to walk or ride into the CBD, so this is as much a rental market as an own-stay one. Yields on the condos sit around 3.3% gross, modest but steady, carried by a constant stream of young professionals who want a central postcode without a prime-district price tag. There is a small HDB pool too, where a four-room sits near a $990k median at about $992 psf and rents around $3,800 a month for a stronger 4.6% yield.

Getting around is the whole point. Lavender is on the East-West Line, with Bendemeer on the Downtown Line and Bugis nearby, so most of the neighbourhood is a short walk from a train and a few stops from the office. Food is everywhere, the Jalan Besar shophouses, the Lavender Food Square area and Little India all within reach, and the Kallang River and Kallang Riverside Park give you what greenery there is. The honest catch is that this is a compact, urban strip. Greenery is limited, much of the stock is small investor units, parts of the Jalan Besar and Kallang fringe are still gritty, and the HDB pool is thin. The nearby Kampong Bugis site is slated for redevelopment, which could reshape the northern edge over time.

The honest view

What works

  • One or two MRT stops from the CBD on the East-West Line, at a clear discount to Marina Bay and Tanjong Pagar prices.
  • Mixed-use condos with shops and food downstairs, plus the Jalan Besar shophouses, Little India and Bugis all within reach.
  • A steady rental market: compact units let investors buy into a central postcode with yields around 3.3% on condos and 4.6% in the HDB pocket.

Worth weighing

  • Dense and urban with limited greenery, so it trades leafy space for location.
  • Much of the stock is compact one and two-bedroom investor units rather than family-sized homes.
  • Parts of the Jalan Besar and Kallang fringe are still gritty, and the HDB pool here is small.
