Novena

Singapore's medical district in District 11, one stop from Orchard, where the draw is a central address and the trade-off is prime pricing with precious little green.

What it's like

Central and well-connected, priced like the prime district it sits in, and built around hospitals and malls more than parks.

Novena is District 11, wrapped around its North-South Line station on Thomson Road, and it wears two hats. The first is medical. Tan Tock Seng Hospital, Mount Elizabeth Novena and a run of specialist clinics anchor the area, and the HealthCity Novena redevelopment is pulling even more of the country's healthcare into one square kilometre. The second is retail and homes: Velocity, Novena Square and United Square sit on top of the station, and private condominiums fill the streets running off Thomson, Moulmein and Newton.

What people pay for here is the location. You are one stop from Newton and two from Orchard on the North-South Line, with Newton interchange onto the Downtown Line next door and the CTE at the doorstep for drivers. That puts town, the CBD and Orchard shopping inside about fifteen minutes without living on Orchard Road itself. The buyers reflect it: doctors and hospital staff who want to be near work, professionals who want central without the Orchard price tag, and families who value being close to the good central schools. It is almost entirely private ground, so a resale condo here runs around $2.58 million, or roughly $2,118 per square foot, and yields sit near 2.9 percent because this is bought to live in and hold.

The honest picture is that Novena is central rather than pretty. Green space is thin and mostly manicured, the pockets around Zhongshan Park and the odd condo grounds rather than a proper nature spot, so if you want a park on your morning run this is not the address. The medical district also brings real daytime traffic and ambulance runs around the hospitals, and some of the condo stock along Thomson and Moulmein is now twenty years old or more, so you are often paying a prime-area price for an older unit. You come to Novena for the central location, the connectivity and the convenience, and you accept that the surroundings are urban and the numbers on a rental spreadsheet are modest.

Who it suitsMedical professionals and central-minded buyers who want to be one stop from town and can absorb prime District 11 pricing for an older or smaller unit, rather than chasing yield or greenery.

The honest view

What works

  • One stop from Newton and two from Orchard on the North-South Line, with the CTE and Newton interchange close by, so town and the CBD are a short hop.
  • Singapore's medical anchor, with Tan Tock Seng, Mount Elizabeth Novena and HealthCity Novena, plus Velocity, Novena Square and United Square on the doorstep.
  • A central District 11 address for less than Orchard or the core prime districts, which is why it holds value with doctors and professionals.

Worth weighing

  • Prime pricing across the board, with the condo median around $2.58m and roughly $2,118 per square foot.
  • Thin, mostly manicured green space and real hospital-district traffic, so it feels urban and clinical rather than leafy.
  • Older condo stock in parts and yields near 2.9 percent, so you often pay a central premium for a modest, aging unit.
