Orchard

Singapore's flagship shopping and tourism spine, and the ultra-prime residential enclaves just off it in Districts 9 and 10, where trophy homes trade well past $10m.

What it's like

The country's most recognised address, bought for prestige and capital preservation rather than for the rental numbers.

Orchard is two things at once. The main belt is Orchard Road itself, the shopping and tourism spine that runs from Tanglin down to Dhoby Ghaut, lined with ION Orchard, Ngee Ann City, Takashimaya, Paragon and the hotels that fill up every school holiday. Step one street back and the character changes completely. The residential enclaves of Cairnhill, Scotts, Ardmore Park, Nassim, Grange and Paterson sit quietly behind the malls, in Districts 9 and 10, and this is where the money actually lives. There is no HDB here at all. The stock is condominiums and a thin layer of landed and Good Class Bungalow ground, and the pricing sits at the top of the national table.

What people buy into is the address. These are long-hold luxury homes, bought for prestige, for a District 9 or 10 postcode, and to park and preserve capital, not to chase rent. Older trophy blocks like Ardmore Park still change hands well past $12m, and even a smaller unit at a name development runs into the millions. The buyers are established local families, business owners and foreign purchasers who can absorb the additional buyer's stamp duty and who measure the decision in decades. Resale volume in the core is genuinely thin, because owners here rarely need to sell, so a listing that fits your brief may simply not exist in the month you are looking.

Getting around is easy. Orchard and Somerset sit on the North-South Line, and Orchard Boulevard on the Thomson-East Coast Line now serves the quieter Grange and Paterson side. Anglo-Chinese School on Barker Road is close, the Istana grounds sit right on the belt for green space, and the Botanic Gardens is a short walk or one stop away. The honest catch is the price of admission and what you get for it. This is expensive ground, the trophy market is illiquid, the main shopping belt carries tourist crowds and weekend traffic, and there are very few normal family-sized options once you leave the luxury towers. Gross yield sits around 2.6%, thin by any measure. You buy in Orchard for the name and the location, not for the spreadsheet.

The honest view

What works

  • The most recognised address in Singapore, in prime Districts 9 and 10, with the shopping belt, hotels and the CBD all close by.
  • Well served by rail, with Orchard and Somerset on the North-South Line and Orchard Boulevard on the Thomson-East Coast Line for the quieter enclaves.
  • Values hold, backed by established local and foreign demand, the Istana grounds and Botanic Gardens for green space, and schools like Anglo-Chinese nearby.

Worth weighing

  • Extremely expensive to buy into, with a condo median around $2.84m and trophy homes trading well past $10m.
  • The core resale market is thin and illiquid, because these are long-hold luxury units that owners rarely need to sell.
  • Yields are low, roughly 2.6% gross, the main belt carries tourist crowds and weekend traffic, and there are very few normal family-sized options.
