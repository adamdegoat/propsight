What it's like

Central and affordable for its postcode, Balestier is a food-led, unpretentious address just off Novena and Toa Payoh, older stock and a bus to the MRT included.

Balestier runs along Balestier Road in District 12, on the city fringe inside the Novena planning area and next door to Whampoa and Toa Payoh. The housing here is private but not prime: rows of older walk-up apartments, many without lifts or facilities, sitting alongside newer small condos such as Soleil at Sinaran, Vista Residences and The Interweave. Actual HDB is close to non-existent on the strip itself, so this reads as a private market, but one priced well below the district's Novena core.

The identity is the food and the heritage. Balestier Road is one of Singapore's best-known eating streets, home to Loy Kee and Boon Tong Kee chicken rice, several bak kut teh houses, and the famous tau sar piah shops that people drive across the island for. Behind the hawker fare sit the old lighting and hardware stores, long rows of conserved shophouses, the Sun Yat Sen Nanyang Memorial Hall, and Zhongshan Park with its mall and hotels. It pulls in singles, couples and investors who want a central address with real character and can live without a big flat or a school catchment.

The honest catch is access and stock. There has never been an MRT station on the strip, so getting around means buses or a walk to Novena on the North-South Line or across to Toa Payoh, and Balestier Road itself carries steady traffic and noise. A lot of the walk-ups are ageing and lift-less, units tend to be compact, and there are few family-sized homes. What you get in return is a central, food-rich postcode at a condo median around $1.70m, roughly $1,766 psf, with gross yields near 3.0%, better value than most addresses this close to town.

The honest view

What works

  • A central, food-rich address at a condo median around $1.70m, well below the Novena core next door.
  • One of Singapore's best eating streets on the doorstep, with chicken rice, bak kut teh and tau sar piah, plus conserved shophouses and the Sun Yat Sen Nanyang Memorial Hall.
  • Gross yields near 3.0%, on the healthier side for a central location, which suits investors and couples buying to live and rent.

Worth weighing

  • No MRT station on the doorstep, so it is bus-reliant, with the nearest stations at Novena and Toa Payoh.
  • A lot of the stock is older walk-up apartments without lifts or facilities, and units tend to be compact with few family-sized homes.
  • Balestier Road carries steady traffic and noise, and this is a lifestyle-and-value buy rather than a prestige address.
