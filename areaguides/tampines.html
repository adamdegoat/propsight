Tampines

What it's like

Tampines runs like a full town in its own right, not a suburb of somewhere else.

Tampines is one of Singapore's three original regional centres, built up through the 1980s and long since fully matured. It runs like a self-contained town rather than a suburb of somewhere else. A dense retail core of four malls, Tampines Mall, Century Square, Tampines 1 and Our Tampines Hub, clusters around the interchange, with a retail park holding IKEA, Courts and Giant a little further out, and rows of hawker centres, wet markets and coffee shops in between. For most of the week you never really need to leave a two-kilometre radius.

The housing is a genuine mix. Large tracts of resale HDB, much of it now 30 to 40 years old with leases in the high 60s to 70s, sit next to a wave of newer private launches like Treasure at Tampines, The Tapestry and The Tampines Trilliant. That spread is why a $665k four-room flat and a $1.55m condo can share the same postal district. It has always been a family town: established primary and secondary schools, big parks and sports facilities, and a settled, unflashy feel rather than a young-professional buzz.

Getting around is easy within the east. Three MRT stations put you on both the East-West and Downtown lines, Changi Airport and the Expo are about ten minutes away, and the PIE and TPE ring the town for drivers. The honest catch is the one it has always had: you sit at the far eastern edge, so the ride into the central business district runs around half an hour, and that distance is priced into both the flats and the daily commute.

Who it suitsFamilies who want space, strong schools and everything within walking distance, and don't mind a longer commute into town.

The honest view

What works

  • A four-room here grosses around 6.1% in rent, among the healthier returns in the east.
  • Genuinely self-sufficient: four malls, Our Tampines Hub, hawker centres and established schools.
  • Well connected within the east, with three MRT stations across two lines.

Worth weighing

  • A real commute into the central districts, this is the far east.
  • Many older flats now have around 69 years of lease left, which matters for financing and a long hold.
  • Condos are a big step up in price for modest yields near 3.7%.
