Ang Mo Kio

One of Singapore's oldest mature towns, a settled heartland estate with everything grown in around the town centre over the MRT, and strong resale yields on the flats.

What it's like

Ang Mo Kio is old-Singapore heartland, fully grown in and priced for it, with high HDB yields and thin, expensive condo stock.

Ang Mo Kio was one of the first HDB new towns, built out from the 1970s, and it feels every bit as settled as that history suggests. The town centre sits directly over Ang Mo Kio MRT, with AMK Hub and Jubilee Square on top of it, the bus interchange next door, and older wet markets, hawker centres and coffee shops filling the blocks around. It is not a showpiece estate. It is a working heartland town where a lot of residents have lived for decades and the amenities are already there rather than promised.

The housing is overwhelmingly HDB, and a lot of it is older stock. Plenty of blocks date to the town's first decades, so leases run shorter than in newer estates, which matters for financing and for anyone planning a long hold. Private supply is limited: a handful of condos like The Panorama, Centro Residences and Grandeur 8 sit near the town centre and off Ang Mo Kio Avenue 8, and they trade at firm prices for fairly modest rental yields. The result is a real split. A four-room resale flat around $612k and a two-million-dollar condo can sit a few streets apart, and they draw different buyers entirely.

For transport, Ang Mo Kio is central-north and well placed. The North-South Line runs straight from the town centre into Orchard and the city in roughly 20 minutes, and the Cross Island Line will add an interchange here, which is a genuine future upside. The honest trade-off is the age of the estate. Parts of it feel dated, the older flats carry shorter leases, and the condo choice is narrow and pricey. You are buying a mature, proven location, not a fresh one.

Who it suitsBuyers who want a proven, central-north heartland location with strong HDB rental yields, and are comfortable with older stock and shorter leases rather than a new estate.

The honest view

What works

  • Rental returns are healthy here, a four-room grosses around 6.7%, helped by the tenant demand a central-north location pulls.
  • Central-north and well connected, roughly 20 minutes into town on the North-South Line, with a Cross Island Line interchange to come.
  • A fully mature town with everything already there: MRT, malls, markets, established schools like CHIJ St Nicholas and Anderson, and the town gardens.

Worth weighing

  • A lot of the flats are older with shorter leases left, which matters for financing and a long hold.
  • Parts of the estate feel dated, with the ageing-town character that comes with age.
  • Condo stock is thin and priced high for modest yields near 2.9%.
