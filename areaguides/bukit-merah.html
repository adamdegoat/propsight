Bukit Merah

A large, very central town just south-west of the CBD, holding conserved Tiong Bahru, Redhill and Alexandra, with a clear central premium built into the prices.

What it's like

Bukit Merah buys you a ten-minute run into town, and you pay for it.

Bukit Merah sits just south-west of the city, close enough that the drive into the CBD runs around ten minutes off-peak. It is a big planning area that takes in several distinct pockets: Tiong Bahru, the conserved 1930s walk-up estate now known for its cafes, bakeries and wet market; Redhill and Alexandra with their older public housing; and Bukit Merah Central, the town's own retail and hawker core. The Tiong Bahru quarter in particular has become one of the most photographed low-rise estates in Singapore, all curved balconies, spiral staircases and a Sunday brunch crowd.

The housing is an unusually wide spread for one town. You get some of the oldest HDB stock in Singapore, conserved pre-war SIT flats, big blocks of 1970s and 1980s public housing, and a run of newer private launches like Avenue South Residence, Alex Residences and Highline Residences. That range is why a $932k four-room flat and a near-$2m condo sit in the same district. Buyers here are mostly people who want to stay close to the city without paying prime-district money, plus a steady stream of younger owners and renters drawn to the Tiong Bahru scene.

Getting around is genuinely easy. The East-West Line runs through Redhill, Tiong Bahru and Queenstown, the Circle Line adds another route, and Mount Faber, Telok Blangah Hill and the Southern Ridges are a short hop for green space. The honest trade-off is the one the location creates: you pay a clear central premium on both flats and condos, many blocks are old with shorter leases left, and the trendy Tiong Bahru pocket is expensive for what is still, on paper, older housing.

Who it suitsBuyers who want to be ten minutes from town and near the Tiong Bahru scene, and will pay a central premium for it rather than move further out.

The honest view

What works

  • About 10 minutes to the CBD, one of the closest towns you can buy in outside the prime districts.
  • Two MRT lines and three East-West stations, with the Circle Line adding a second route into town.
  • The Tiong Bahru estate, its market and cafes, plus the Southern Ridges and Mount Faber close by.

Worth weighing

  • You pay a clear central premium, a four-room here runs well above what the same flat fetches further out.
  • Much of the HDB stock is old, with shorter leases left, which matters for financing and a long hold.
  • Condo yields are thin, near 2.7%, and the trendy Tiong Bahru pocket is expensive for older housing.
