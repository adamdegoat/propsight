What it's like

Whampoa is where you buy a settled, food-led HDB address close to the city without paying condo money.

Whampoa is one of the older parts of the Kallang/Whampoa town, a mature HDB estate laid out along the Whampoa River in the middle of the island. The St George's, Bendemeer and Jalan Bahagia blocks give it a settled, lived-in feel, and the thing that defines the neighbourhood is food. Whampoa Drive Makan Place, the hawker centre most people just call the Whampoa food centre, is an institution, and the wet market beside it pulls in cooks from well beyond the estate. It is an unpretentious, everyday address that happens to sit a short ride from town.

The housing is almost all HDB, and it is priced like the central town it belongs to. A four-room resale runs around $914k, near $910 psf, which is well above the outer towns and buys you the location rather than space. Private stock is thin and small-scale: a handful of compact condos like Montebleu, The Axis, The Interweave and Prestige Heights, most of them modest projects rather than the big riverside developments you find nearer Kallang. Those condos sit around $1.37m, near $1,690 psf. The buyers here are mostly families and value hunters who want a central, well-connected HDB flat, not investors chasing a large private upside.

Getting around leans on Boon Keng on the North-East Line, the nearest station, with buses running along Balestier and Serangoon Roads into town and out to the wider island. It is genuinely central, so the city is a quick trip, and the Whampoa River park connector gives the estate a green edge to walk or cycle. The honest trade-offs are the age of the place: many blocks are older with shorter remaining leases, the MRT is a walk rather than on your doorstep, the private choice is limited and small, and it is a dense estate rather than a roomy suburb. You are paying for a central, food-led location, and on that it delivers.

The honest view

What works

  • A mature, central HDB address at good value for how close it sits to town, with a four-room resale around $914k.
  • Whampoa Drive Makan Place and the wet market on the doorstep, one of the better-known hawker spots in the country.
  • Boon Keng on the North-East Line and buses along Balestier and Serangoon Roads put the city within a short ride, with the Whampoa River park connector for green space.

Worth weighing

  • Many flats are older, with the shorter remaining leases that come with a long-established estate.
  • The nearest MRT is a walk away at Boon Keng, so it is less convenient than a town built around its own station.
  • Private choice is very limited and small in scale, and the estate is dense rather than spacious.
